###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> At that time, the disciples came to Jesus and asked, “Who then is greatest in the Kingdom of Heaven?” ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Jesus called a little child to himself and set him in their midst. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> He then said, “Amen, I tell you that unless you change and become as little children, you will in no way enter into the Kingdom of Heaven. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> But whoever humbles himself as this little child is the greatest in the Kingdom of Heaven. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Whoever receives such a little child in my Name receives me, ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> but whoever causes one of these little ones who believe in me to stumble, it would be better for such a person to have a huge millstone hung around the neck and be drowned in the depths of the sea! ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Woe to the world because of occasions of stumbling! Such occasions are bound to come, but woe to the one through whom the occasion does come! ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> If your hand or your foot causes you to stumble, cut them off and throw them away from you! It is better for you to enter into life crippled or maimed rather than to have two hands or two feet and yet be cast into eternal fire. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> If your eye causes you to stumble, pluck it out and throw it away from you. It is better for you to enter into life with one eye, rather than to be cast into the Gehenna <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn244|†]]</sup> of fire having two eyes. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Do not despise any of these little ones, for I tell you that in heaven, their angels always see the face of my Father who is in heaven. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> <For the Son of Man has come to save the lost.> <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn245|†]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> What do you think? If a man has a hundred sheep and one of them goes astray, does he not leave the ninety-nine to go into the mountains and look for the one that has gone astray? ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Amen, I tell you: if he finds it, he rejoices over this one more than over the ninety-nine which did not go astray. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Likewise, it is not the will <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn246|†]]</sup> of your Father who is in heaven that any of these little ones should perish. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> If your brother sins against you, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn247|†]]</sup> go, show him his fault between you and him alone. If he listens to you, you have gained back your brother! ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> But if he does not listen, take one or two more with you, so that at the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn248|†]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> If he refuses to listen to them, tell it to the Church. If he refuses to hear the Church also, let him be to you as a Gentile or a tax collector. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> Amen, I tell you <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn249|†]]</sup> that whatever you bind on earth will have been bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will have been loosed in heaven. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Again, amen, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn250|†]]</sup> I tell you that if two of you will agree on earth concerning anything they will ask, it will be done for them by my Father who is in heaven. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Indeed, where two or three are gathered together in my Name, there I am among them.” <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn251|†]]</sup> ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Then Peter came forward and asked Jesus, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn252|†]]</sup> “Lord, how often shall I forgive my brother who sins against me? Until seven times?” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Jesus said to him, “I do not tell you until seven times, but seventy times seven! ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Therefore, the Kingdom of Heaven is like a king who wanted to settle his accounts with his slaves. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> When he began the settlement, someone was brought in who owed him ten thousand talents. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn253|†]]</sup> ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> But because the slave <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn254|†]]</sup> could not pay, his lord gave orders that he be sold, with his wife, children, and all that he had, so that payment may be made. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> At this, the slave fell down on his knees before him, saying: ‘Lord, have patience with me, and I will repay you all!’ ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> The lord of that slave, moved with compassion, released him, and forgave him the debt. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> However, that slave went out and found one of his fellow slaves who owed him one hundred denarii. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn255|†]]</sup> He grabbed him and took him by the throat, saying: ‘Pay me what you owe!’ ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> And so, his fellow slave fell down at his feet and begged him, saying: ‘Have patience with me, and I will repay you!’ ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> But the first man would not [have compassion] but went off and had him cast into prison, until he should pay back what was due. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> When his [other] fellow slaves saw what had been done, they were extremely distressed and came to their lord to report all that had taken place. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Then his lord summoned the [first] slave and said to him, ‘You wicked slave! I forgave you all that debt because you begged me. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Should you not also have had mercy on your fellow slave, even as I had mercy on you?’ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> His lord was angry and delivered him to the jailers <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — EOB Notes#^edn256|†]]</sup> until he would pay all that was due. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> This is how my heavenly Father will also treat you if you do not each forgive each other their trespasses from your hearts.” ^v35

###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> There was a man from Ramathaim Zophim from Mount Ephraim, and his name was Elkanah son of Jeroham, son of Elihu, son of Tohu, in Nasib of Ephraim. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And this man had two wives; the name of one was Hannah, and the name of the second, Peninnah. And Peninnah had children, and Hannah had no child. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And the man regularly went up from time to time from his town, from Ramah, to worship and sacrifice to the Lord God Sabaoth to Shiloh. And there Eli and his two sons, Hophni and Phinehas, were priests of the Lord. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And it was on that day, Elkanah sacrificed and gave to Peninnah, his wife, and her sons a portion; ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> and to Hannah he gave one portion because she had no child; except that Elkanah loved Hannah more than his other wife, and the Lord kept her womb closed, ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> because the Lord did not give her a child despite her anguish and despite the hopelessness of her anguish. She lost heart because of this: that the Lord kept her womb closed by not giving her a child. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> This is how she behaved year after year when she would go up to the house of the Lord: She was disheartened and wept and did not eat. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> And Elkanah, her husband, said to her, “Hannah.” And she said to him, “Here I am, lord.”<sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — Lexham Notes#^v8|*]]</sup> And he said to her, “What is it to you that you are weeping? And why are you not eating, and why do you beat your heart? Am I not good to you beyond ten children?” ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And she rose after they ate in Shiloh and stood before the Lord, and Eli the priest was on a chair at the entryway of the shrine of the Lord. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And she was grieved in her soul and prayed to the Lord and wept. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> And she prayed in her prayer to the Lord, saying, “Adonai, Lord Eloai Sabaoth, if you are looking, you are looking upon the humiliated state of your slave. Remember me and give your slave seed of men, and I will give him to you as a devoted one until the day of his death. And he will not drink wine and intoxicating drink, and a razor will not go over his head.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And it happened that while she multiplied her prayers before the Lord, Eli the priest watched her mouth. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> She was speaking in her heart and her lips were moving, but her voice was not heard. So Eli thought she was drunk. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> And Eli’s young servant said to her, “How long will you be drunk? Get rid of your wine and go out from the Lord’s face!” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And Hannah answered and said, “No, sir! I am a woman who has had a hard day. And I have not drank wine and intoxicating drink, and I am pouring out my soul before the Lord. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Do not regard your slave as a pestilent daughter because I have been stretching out my great number of words until now.” ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And Eli answered and said to her, “Go in peace. May the God of Israel give you your entire request that you requested from him.” ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And she said, “May your slave find favor in your eyes.” And the woman went on her way and entered into her lodging and ate with her husband and drank. And her face no longer fell. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And they rose early and worshiped the Lord and went on their way. Elkanah entered his house at Ramah and knew Hannah, his wife, and the Lord remembered her. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> And she conceived, and it happened at the time of the days she bore a son and called his name Samuel. She said, “Because from the Lord God Sabaoth I asked for him.” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> And the man, Elkanah, and his entire house went up to sacrifice at Shiloh for the annual sacrifice and his vow and the entire tithe from his land. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Hannah did not go up with him because she said to her husband, “Until the child goes up, I will wean him, and then he will be seen by the face of the Lord, and he will live there until eternity.” ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And Elkanah, her husband, said to her, “Do what you view as right. Stay until you might wean him, but may the Lord establish the words that come out of your mouth.” And the woman stayed and suckled her son until she might wean him. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And she went up with him to Shiloh with a three-year-old calf and bread and an ephah<sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — Lexham Notes#^v24|*]]</sup> of wheat flour and a jar of wine. And she entered the house of the Lord at Shiloh, and the child was with them. They brought him before the Lord, and his father killed the sacrifice, which he did from year to year to the Lord, and brought the child. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And he slaughtered the calf, and Hannah, the mother of the child, brought him to Eli. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> And she said, “By me, Lord, as your soul lives, I am the woman who stood before you with you in prayer to the Lord. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> For this is the child I prayed for, and the Lord gave my request that I asked from him. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And I have given him to the Lord all the days that he lives to be used by the Lord.” ^v28

###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> So now: no condemnation for those in the Anointed One, Jesus. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> For the law of the spirit of life in the Anointed One Jesus freed you from the law of sin and death. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> For the thing that is impossible for the Law, in which it was weak on account of the flesh—God, having sent his own Son in a semblance of the flesh of sin, also (as regards sin) condemned sin in the flesh, ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> In order that the Law’s just ordinance might be fulfilled in us who walk not according to the flesh but according to spirit. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> For those who exist according to flesh incline the mind to the things of the flesh; but those according to spirit, the things of the spirit. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> For the mental inclination of the flesh is death; but the mental inclination of the spirit is life and peace; ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Hence the mental inclination of the flesh is enmity to God; for it is not subordinated to the Law of God, nor indeed can it be; ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> For those existing in flesh cannot be pleasing to God. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> But you are not in flesh, but rather in spirit, since God’s Spirit dwells in you. But if one does not have the Spirit of the Anointed, this one is not his. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> But, if the Anointed is in you, the body is dead on account of sin, and yet the spirit<sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — DBH Notes#^fn236|†]]</sup> is life on account of uprightness. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> But if the Spirit of the one who has raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, he who raised the Anointed One Jesus from the dead will also make your mortal bodies live through the indwelling of his Spirit in you. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> So then, brothers, we are indebted to the flesh, to live according to flesh. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> For if you live according to the flesh, you are about to die; but, if you put the deeds of the body to death by spirit, you will live. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> For as many as are led by God’s Spirit, these are God’s sons. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> For you did not receive again a spirit of slavery to fear, but rather received a spirit of adoption in which we cry, “Abba, Father!” ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> The Spirit itself testifies along with our spirit that we are God’s children. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And, if children, heirs as well; on the one hand, God’s heirs and, on the other, co-heirs of the Anointed, since we co-suffer in order that we may be co-glorified. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> For I reckon the sufferings of the present time to be of no worth before the coming glory that will be revealed to us. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> For the earnest expectation of creation anxiously awaits the revelation of the sons of God. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For creation was made subordinate to pointlessness, not willingly but because of the one who subordinated it, in the hope ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> That creation itself will also be liberated from decay into the freedom of the glory of God’s children. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> For we know that all creation groans together and labors together in birth pangs, up to this moment; ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Not only this, but even we ourselves, having the firstfruits of the spirit, groan within ourselves as well, anxiously awaiting adoption, emancipation of our body. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> For in hope we have been saved, but a hope seen is not hope; for why hope for what one sees? ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> But, if we hope for what we do not see, we anticipate by perseverance. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> And likewise the spirit also gives us aid in our infirmity; for we do not know what we ought to pray for, but the spirit itself makes intercession with unutterable groans, ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> And he who searches out the heart knows what the spirit’s mind is, for in accord with God it makes intercession on behalf of the holy ones. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And we know that, for those loving God, all things work together for good to those called according to a purpose. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> Because those he knew in advance he then<sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — DBH Notes#^fn235|†]]</sup> marked out in advance as being in conformity<sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — DBH Notes#^fn234|†]]</sup> to the image of his Son, so that he might be firstborn among many brothers;<sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — DBH Notes#^fn233|†]]</sup> ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And those he marked out in advance, these he then called; those he called, these he then proved righteous;<sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — DBH Notes#^fn232|†]]</sup> and those he proved righteous, these he then glorified. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> What then shall we say about these things? If God is for us, who is against us? He who did not even spare his own Son, ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> But rather delivered him over on behalf of all of us, how shall he not grace us with all things along with him? ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Who will make an accusation against God’s chosen ones? God is the one who vindicates: ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> Who is the one who condemns? The Anointed One, Jesus? He who has died—or, rather, he who was raised? Who is at God’s right hand? Who also intercedes for us? ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> Who will separate us from the love of the Anointed? Affliction or anguish or persecution or famine or nakedness or peril or the sword? ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> As has been written: “For your sake we are being put to death all day long, we were reckoned as sheep for slaughter.” ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> Rather, in all these things we more than conquer through the one who has loved us. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> For I have been persuaded that neither death nor life nor angels nor Archons<sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — DBH Notes#^fn231|†]]</sup> nor things present nor things imminent nor Powers, ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Nor height nor depth nor any other creature will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in the Anointed, Jesus our Lord. ^v39

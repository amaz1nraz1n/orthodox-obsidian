### [[Lamentations 1#v1|1:1]]
^v1
> [!note] 1:1 ^f3843
> **The city** is Jerusalem, once **full of people** but now in captivity in Babylon.

### [[Lamentations 1#v5|1:5]]
^v5
> [!note] 1:5 ^f3844
> During Lent we pray, “O Lord and Master of our lives . . .” Here, the **oppressors have become the master**. This captivity is the result of the **ungodliness** of God's people. A stark picture of life outside of fellowship with God.

### [[Lamentations 1#v7|1:7]]
^v7
> [!note] 1:7 ^f3845
> **Jerusalem remembers** tells us the author is looking back to the fall of the city and the grief of **her people** as they endured **deportation** to Babylon.

### [[Lamentations 1#v8|1:8]]
^v8
> [!note] 1:8 ^f3846
> **Jerusalem sinned grievously** sums up the cause of God's discipline of His people.

### [[Lamentations 1#v11|1:11]]
^v11
> [!note] 1:11 ^f3847
> Tragically, the phrase **the objects of their desire for food** is a reference (as in [[Lamentations 2#v4|2:4]]) to the eating of their own children **to stay alive**. We ask incredulously, how could they possibly do that? But remember: in our time, we do not eat them; we abort them.

### [[Lamentations 1#v18|1:18]]
^v18
> [!note] 1:18 ^f3848
> **I provoked His mouth** is a reference to breaking the laws God spoke to Moses and thus to His people. From [[Lamentations 1#v18|1:18–22]], the “I” is the city of Jerusalem, and the warning for her disobedience was issued and recorded in [[Deuteronomy 28#v15|Dt 28:15–68]].

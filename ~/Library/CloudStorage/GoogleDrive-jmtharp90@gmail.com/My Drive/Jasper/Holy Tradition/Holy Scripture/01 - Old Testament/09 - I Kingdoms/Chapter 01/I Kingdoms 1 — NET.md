*Hannah Is Childless*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> There was a man from Ramathaim Zophim, <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090011|‡]]</sup> from the hill country of Ephraim. His name was Elkanah. He was the son of Jeroham, the son of Elihu, the son of Tohu, the son of Zuph, an Ephraimite. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> He had two wives; <sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090012|†]]</sup> the name of the first was Hannah and the name of the second was Peninnah. Peninnah had children, but Hannah had no children. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> This man would go up from his city year after year <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090013|*]]</sup> to worship and to sacrifice to the Lord of Heaven’s Armies at Shiloh. <sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090014|†]]</sup> (It was there that the two sons of Eli, <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090015|‡]]</sup> Hophni and Phinehas, served as the Lord ’s priests.) ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> The day came, and Elkanah sacrificed. (Now <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090016|*]]</sup> he used to give meat portions to his wife Peninnah and to all her sons and daughters. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> But to Hannah he would give a double <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090017|*]]</sup> portion because he loved Hannah, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090018|*]]</sup> although <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n090019|*]]</sup> the Lord had not enabled her to have children. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Her rival used to aggravate her to the point of exasperation, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900110|*]]</sup> just to irritate her, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900111|*]]</sup> since the Lord had not enabled her to have children. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> This is how it would go <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900112|‡]]</sup> year after year. As often as she went up to the Lord ’s house, Peninnah <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900113|*]]</sup> would offend her in that way.) <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900114|*]]</sup> So she cried and refused to eat. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Then her husband Elkanah said to her, “Hannah, why are you crying and why won’t you eat? Why are you so upset? <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900115|*]]</sup> Am I not better to you than ten <sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900116|†]]</sup> sons?” ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> So Hannah got up after they had finished eating and drinking in Shiloh. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900117|‡]]</sup> At the time <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900118|*]]</sup> Eli the priest was sitting in his chair <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900119|*]]</sup> by the doorpost of the Lord ’s sanctuary. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900120|*]]</sup> ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> As for Hannah, she was very distressed. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900121|*]]</sup> She prayed to the Lord and was, in fact, weeping. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900122|*]]</sup> ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> She made a vow saying, “O Lord of Heaven’s Armies, if you would truly look <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900123|*]]</sup> on the suffering of your servant, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900124|*]]</sup> and would keep me in mind and not neglect <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900125|‡]]</sup> your servant, and give your servant a male child, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900126|*]]</sup> then I will dedicate him to the Lord all the days of his life. His hair will never be cut.” <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900127|‡]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> It turned out <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900128|*]]</sup> that she did a great deal <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900129|*]]</sup> of praying before the Lord. Meanwhile <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900130|*]]</sup> Eli was watching her mouth. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> As for Hannah, she was speaking in her mind. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900131|*]]</sup> Only her lips were moving; her voice could not be heard. So Eli thought she was a drunkard. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900132|*]]</sup> ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Then he <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900133|‡]]</sup> said to her, “How much longer do you intend to get drunk? Put away your wine!” <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900134|‡]]</sup> ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> But Hannah replied, “Not so, my lord! I am a woman under a great deal of stress. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900135|*]]</sup> I haven’t drunk wine or beer. But I have poured out my soul before the Lord. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Don’t consider your servant a wicked woman. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900136|*]]</sup> It’s just that, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900137|*]]</sup> to this point, I have spoken from my deep pain <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900138|*]]</sup> and anguish.” <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900139|*]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Eli replied, “Go in peace, and may the God of Israel grant the request that you have asked of him.” ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> She said, “May I, your servant, find favor in your sight.” <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900140|‡]]</sup> So the woman went her way and got something to eat. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900141|‡]]</sup> Her face no longer looked sad. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900142|‡]]</sup> ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> They got up early the next morning. Then they worshiped <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900143|*]]</sup> the Lord and returned to their home at Ramathaim. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900144|‡]]</sup> Elkanah was intimate with <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900145|*]]</sup> his wife Hannah, and the Lord called her to mind. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900146|*]]</sup> ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Then Hannah became pregnant. In the course of time she gave birth to a son. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900147|‡]]</sup> And she named him Samuel, thinking, “I asked the Lord for him.” <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900149|*]]</sup> ^v20

*Hannah Dedicates Samuel to the Lord*
###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Then the man Elkanah and all his family went up to make the yearly sacrifice <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900150|*]]</sup> to the Lord and to keep his vow. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900151|‡]]</sup> ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> But Hannah did not go up with them, <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900152|*]]</sup> because she had told <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900153|*]]</sup> her husband, “Not <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900154|*]]</sup> until the boy is weaned. Then I will bring him so that he may appear before the Lord. And he will remain there from then on.” <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900155|*]]</sup> ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Then her husband Elkanah said to her, “Do what you think best. <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900156|*]]</sup> Stay until you have weaned him. Only may the Lord fulfill his promise.” <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900157|‡]]</sup> So the woman stayed and nursed her son until she had weaned him. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Then she took him up with her <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900158|‡]]</sup> as soon as she had weaned him, along with three bulls, <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900159|‡]]</sup> an ephah <sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900160|†]]</sup> of flour, and a container <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900161|*]]</sup> of wine. She came to the Lord ’s house at Shiloh, and the boy was with them. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900162|‡]]</sup> ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> They slaughtered the bull, then brought the boy to Eli. <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900163|‡]]</sup> ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> She said, “My lord. Just as surely as you are alive, my lord, I am the woman who previously stood here with you in order to pray to the Lord. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> For this boy I prayed, and the Lord has given me the request that I asked of him. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> So I also dedicate <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900164|*]]</sup> him to the Lord. For all the days of his life <sup class="nt-tn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900165|*]]</sup> he is dedicated to the Lord.” Then he <sup class="nt-tc">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900166|‡]]</sup> bowed down there in worship <sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — NET Notes#^n0900167|†]]</sup> to the Lord. ^v28

*Circumcision in Antioch*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And certain men came down from Judea and taught the brethren, “Unless you are circumcised according to the custom of Moses, you cannot be saved.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5098|†]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Therefore, when Paul and Barnabas had no small dissension and dispute with them, they determined that Paul and Barnabas and certain others of them should go up to Jerusalem, to the apostles and elders, about this question.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5099|†]]</sup> ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> So, being sent on their way by the church, they passed through Phoenicia and Samaria, describing the conversion of the Gentiles; and they caused great joy to all the brethren. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And when they had come to Jerusalem, they were received by the church and the apostles and the elders; and they reported all things that God had done with them. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> But some of the sect of the Pharisees who believed rose up, saying, “It is necessary to circumcise them, and to command them to keep the law of Moses.” ^v5

*The Council in Jerusalem*
###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Now the apostles and elders came together to consider this matter.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5100|†]]</sup> ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> And when there had been much dispute, Peter rose up and said to them: “Men and brethren, you know that a good while ago God chose among us, that by my mouth the Gentiles should hear the word of the gospel and believe.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5101|†]]</sup> ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> So God, who knows the heart, acknowledged them by giving them the Holy Spirit, just as He did to us, ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> and made no distinction between us and them, purifying their hearts by faith. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Now therefore, why do you test God by putting a yoke on the neck of the disciples which neither our fathers nor we were able to bear? ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> But we believe that through the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar451|‡]]</sup> we shall be saved in the same manner as they.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Then all the multitude kept silent and listened to Barnabas and Paul declaring how many miracles and wonders God had worked through them among the Gentiles. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And after they had become silent, James answered, saying, “Men and brethren, listen to me:<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5102|†]]</sup> ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Simon has declared how God at the first visited the Gentiles to take out of them a people for His name. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And with this the words of the prophets agree, just as it is written: ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> ‘After this I will return And will rebuild the tabernacle of David, which has fallen down; I will rebuild its ruins, And I will set it up; ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> So that the rest of mankind may seek the L ORD , Even all the Gentiles who are called by My name, Says the L ORD who does all these things.’<sup class="nt-cross">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fcross175|§]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> “Known to God from eternity are all His works.<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar452|‡]]</sup> ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Therefore I judge that we should not trouble those from among the Gentiles who are turning to God, ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> but that we write to them to abstain from things polluted by idols, from sexual immorality,<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar453|‡]]</sup> from things strangled, and from blood. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> For Moses has had throughout many generations those who preach him in every city, being read in the synagogues every Sabbath.” ^v21

*The Decree: Early Canon Law*
###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Then it pleased the apostles and elders, with the whole church, to send chosen men of their own company to Antioch with Paul and Barnabas, namely, Judas who was also named Barsabas,<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar454|‡]]</sup> and Silas, leading men among the brethren.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5103|†]]</sup> ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> They wrote this, letter by them: The apostles, the elders, and the brethren, To the brethren who are of the Gentiles in Antioch, Syria, and Cilicia: Greetings. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Since we have heard that some who went out from us have troubled you with words, unsettling your souls, saying, “ You must be circumcised and keep the law”<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar455|‡]]</sup> —to whom we gave no such commandment— ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> it seemed good to us, being assembled with one accord, to send chosen men to you with our beloved Barnabas and Paul, ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> men who have risked their lives for the name of our Lord Jesus Christ. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> We have therefore sent Judas and Silas, who will also report the same things by word of mouth. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> For it seemed good to the Holy Spirit, and to us, to lay upon you no greater burden than these necessary things:<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5104|†]]</sup> ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> that you abstain from things offered to idols, from blood, from things strangled, and from sexual immorality.<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar456|‡]]</sup> If you keep yourselves from these, you will do well. Farewell. ^v29

*The Decree Received in Antioch*
###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> So when they were sent off, they came to Antioch; and when they had gathered the multitude together, they delivered the letter. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> When they had read it, they rejoiced over its encouragement. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Now Judas and Silas, themselves being prophets also, exhorted and strengthened the brethren with many words. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And after they had stayed there for a time, they were sent back with greetings from the brethren to the apostles.<sup class="nt-tc">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fvar457|‡]]</sup> ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> However, it seemed good to Silas to remain there.<sup class="nt-cross">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fcross176|§]]</sup> ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> Paul and Barnabas also remained in Antioch, teaching and preaching the word of the Lord, with many others also. ^v35

*Paul and Barnabas Separate*
###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> Then after some days Paul said to Barnabas, “Let us now go back and visit our brethren in every city where we have preached the word of the Lord, and see how they are doing.”<sup class="nt-cit">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^fcit209|¶]]</sup> ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> Now Barnabas was determined to take with them John called Mark.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — OSB Notes#^f5105|†]]</sup> ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> But Paul insisted that they should not take with them the one who had departed from them in Pamphylia, and had not gone with them to the work. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Then the contention became so sharp that they parted from one another. And so Barnabas took Mark and sailed to Cyprus; ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> but Paul chose Silas and departed, being commended by the brethren to the grace of God. ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> And he went through Syria and Cilicia, strengthening the churches. ^v41

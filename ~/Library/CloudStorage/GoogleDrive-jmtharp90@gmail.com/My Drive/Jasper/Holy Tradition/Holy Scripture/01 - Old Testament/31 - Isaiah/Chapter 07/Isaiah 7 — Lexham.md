###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And this happened in the days of Ahaz, the son of Jotham, the son of Uzziah, king of Judah: Rezin, king of Aram, went up with Pekah, son of Remaliah, king of Israel, to Jerusalem to fight against it, and they were not able to besiege it. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And it was reported to the house of David, saying, “Aram has come to an agreement with Ephraim!” And his soul was confounded, along with the soul of his people, the way a tree in a forest is shaken by the wind. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And the Lord said to Isaiah, “Go out for a meeting with Ahaz, you and your remaining son Jashub, to the reservoir of the high road of the fuller’s field. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And you shall say to him, ‘Be careful to be still and do not be frightened or let your soul be weak because of these two smoking firebrand timbers, for when my wrathful anger comes, I will heal again. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And as for the son of Aram and the son of Remaliah, because they planned an evil plan: ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> “We shall go up into Judea and, talking with them, we shall return them to us, and we shall rule it by the son of Tabeel.” ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Thus says the Lord Sabaoth, “This plan will certainly not stand nor will it be. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> But the head of Aram is Damascus, and the head of Damascus is Rezin; and yet within sixty-five years, the kingdom of Ephraim will cease being a people. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And the head of Ephraim is Samaria, and the head of Samaria is the son of Remaliah, and if you do not trust, neither will you understand.” ’ ” ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And the Lord continued to speak to Ahaz, saying, ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> “Ask for yourself a sign from the Lord your God, up to a depth or up to a height.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And Ahaz said, “I will not ask, and I will not test the Lord.” ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And he said, “Hear now, house of David! Is it not a small thing for you to put up a fight with human? And how do you put up a fight with the Lord? ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Because of this, the Lord himself will give you a sign: Look, the virgin will conceive in the womb and will bear a son, and you will call his name Immanuel. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> He will eat butter and honey before he knows either to prefer evil or choose the good.” ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Because before the child knows good or bad, he resists wickedness to choose good; and the land that you fear because of the face of the two kings will be forsaken. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> But God will bring upon you and upon your people and upon the house of your father days that have not yet come since the day when Ephraim took away the king of the Assyrians from Judah. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And this will happen in that day: the Lord will whistle for flies, a swarm of which will be master over part of the river of Egypt, and for the bee that is in the territory of the Assyrians. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And they all will come among the ravines of the territory and in the caves of the cliffs and in the caverns and in every fissure. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> In that day the Lord will shave the head of the king of the Assyrians with the hired razor across the river, and he will remove the hair of the feet and the beard. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> And this will happen: In that day a person will raise a heifer of cattle and two sheep. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> And this will happen: Because of the most abundant production of milk, every one remaining on the land will eat butter and honey. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And this will happen in that day: Every place where there might be a thousand vines worth a thousand shekels will become a dry place and a thistle. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> They will enter there with dart and arrow because the whole land will be a barren place and a thistle. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And every cultivatable hill will be cultivated. Fear will not come upon that place, for it will change from a barren place and a thistle into a sheep pasture and an ox’s trampling ground. ^v25

### [[Isaiah 7#v1|Is 7:1]]
^v1

> [!cite] Bishop Nikolai Velimirovic — Prolog, 13 August
> BEHOLD, THE VIRGIN SHALL CONCEIVE AND BEAR A SON
>
> This glorious prophecy of the Lord’s 
> birth of a virgin was spoken by the Prophet 
> Isaiah, the prophet who saw God, at a mo- 
> ment when Jerusalem found itself in deep 
> despair. The multitudinous army of Syria 
> and Ephraim was entrenched all round 
> the city walls ..Why did the prophet, at 
> that hour and on that occasion, speak the 
> prophecy of the birth of the Savior? Be- 
> cause the human race would, at the time 
> of the coming of the Savior, be in a like 
> despair, run upon and encircled by 
> demonic powers as Jerusalem was at that 
> time. Did the prophet explicitly say 
> ‘virgin’ and not ‘woman’? Obviously he
>
> - said ‘virgin,’ for, if the prophecy had been 
> of a woman, how would it have been a wonder and a sign? Are not all men 
> born of woman? There is great emphasis on the word ‘virgin.’
>
> Thus the all-sceing God knows how to link the near with the far, and to 
> make the fulfilling of one prophecy in the present give emphasis to another 
> in the future. Emmanuel--God with us--saved Jerusalem at that time as 
> God invisible. Emmanuel--God with us-- will save the human race in 
> similar danger later on, as God incarnate, the God-Man, born of the most 
> pure Virgin and the Holy Spirit.
>
> Bishop Nikolai Velimirovic. Prolog, 13 August.

> [!cite] John Chrysostom — Homily I on Hebrews 1, 1
> GOD HAS, AT THE END OF DAYS, 
> SPOKEN TO US BY HIS SON
>
> The expression, ‘in times past,’ and 
> this, ‘in the end of the days,’ shadows 
> forth some other meaning: that when a 
> long time had intervened, when we were 
> on the edge of punishment, when the gifts 
> had failed, when there was no expectation 
> of deliverance, when we were expecting 
> to have less than all - then we have had 
> more.
>
> St. John Chrysostom. Homily I on Hebrews I, 1.

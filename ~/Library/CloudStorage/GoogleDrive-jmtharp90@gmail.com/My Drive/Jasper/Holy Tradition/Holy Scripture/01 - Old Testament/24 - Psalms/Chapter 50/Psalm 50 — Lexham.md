###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> <sup class="nt-tn">[[Psalms 50 — Lexham Notes#^v1|*]]</sup> FOR THE END, A PSALM OF DAVID, ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> WHEN NATHAN THE PROPHET CAME TO HIM WHEN HE HAD GONE INTO BATHSHEBA. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> Have mercy on me, O God, according to your great mercy. According to the magnitude of your compassions blot out my iniquity. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Wash me thoroughly from my lawlessness, and cleanse me from my sin. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> For I know my lawlessness, and my sin is always before me. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Against you alone did I sin, and I did the evil deed before you, so that you would be vindicated in your words and prevail when you judge. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> For look, in evils I was conceived, and in sins my mother conceived me. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> For look, you loved truth. You showed to me the unknown things and the secret things of your wisdom. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Purify me with hyssop, and I will be cleansed. Wash me, and I will be made more white than snow. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Cause me to hear great joy and cheer. The bones, having been made low, will rejoice exceedingly. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> Turn your face away from my sins, and blot out all my lawless acts. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit in my inward parts. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Do not cast me from your face, and do not remove your Holy Spirit from me. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Restore to me the great joy of your salvation, and strengthen me with a guiding spirit. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> I will teach the lawless ones your ways, and the ungodly ones will return to you. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Rescue me from bloodshed, O God, the God of my salvation. My tongue will rejoice exceedingly in your righteousness. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> O Lord, open my lips, and my mouth will declare your praise. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> For if you had wanted a sacrifice, I would have given a whole burnt offering. But you did not want it. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> A sacrifice to God is a broken spirit. A broken and humbled heart God will not disdain. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Do good, O Lord, in your goodwill to Zion, and let the city walls of Jerusalem be restored. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Then you will be well pleased with righteous sacrifice, with offering and whole burnt offerings. Then they will offer up young bulls upon your altar. ^v21

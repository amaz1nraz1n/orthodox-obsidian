###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> If I speak in the tongues of human beings and of the angels, but do not have love, I have become resounding brass and a clanging cymbal. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And if I have prophecy and know all the mysteries and all the knowledge,<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Corinthians 13 — DBH Notes#^fn191|†]]</sup> and if I have all faith, of such a sort as to remove mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And if I distribute all my possessions, and if I hand over my body so that I may be burned, and do not have love, I am profited nothing. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Love is magnanimous, love is kind, is not envious, love does not boast, does not bluster, ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Does not act in an unseemly fashion, does not seek for things of its own, is not irascible, does not take account of the evil deed, ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Does not rejoice in injustice, but rejoices with the truth; ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> It tolerates all things, has faith in all things, hopes in all things, endures all things. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Love never fails; but if there are prophecies, they will be made ineffectual; if tongues, they will cease; if knowledge, it will be made ineffectual. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> For we know partially and we prophesy partially; ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> But, when that which is complete comes, what is partial will be rendered futile. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> When I was an infant, I spoke like an infant, I thought like an infant, I reckoned like an infant; having become a man, I did away with infantile things. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> For as yet we see by way of a mirror, in an enigma, but then face to face; as yet I know partially, but then I shall know fully, just as I am fully known. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> But now abide faith, hope, love—these three—and the greatest of these is love. ^v13

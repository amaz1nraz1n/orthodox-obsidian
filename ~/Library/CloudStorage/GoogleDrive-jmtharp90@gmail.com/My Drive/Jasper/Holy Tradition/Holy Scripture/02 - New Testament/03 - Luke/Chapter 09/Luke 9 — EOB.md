###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Calling his twelve disciples <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn753|†]]</sup> together, Jesus gave them power and authority over all demons, and [power] to cure diseases. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> He then sent them forth to preach the Kingdom of God and to heal the sick. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> Jesus said to them, “Do not take anything for the journey—no staffs, no bag, no bread, no money, no extra tunic. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Whatever house you enter, stay there until you leave from that place. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Wherever people do not welcome you, shake the very dust off your feet when you leave their town, as a testimony against them.” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> They departed and went throughout the villages, preaching the Good News, and healing everywhere. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Now Herod the tetrarch heard of all that was done by Jesus. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn754|†]]</sup> He was very perplexed, because it was said by some that John had risen from the dead. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Some said that Elias (Elijah) had appeared, and others that one of the old prophets had risen again. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Herod said, “I have beheaded John, but who is this about whom I hear such things?” And he desired to see Jesus. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> When they returned, the apostles told him what things they had done. Then Jesus took them with him, and they withdrew to a deserted place [which was part] of a city called Bethsaida. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn755|†]]</sup> ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> However, the crowd perceived this and followed him. Jesus <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn756|†]]</sup> received them, speaking to them about the Kingdom of God, and curing those who needed healing. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Late in the afternoon, the Twelve came to him and said, “Send the people away, so that they may go into the surrounding villages and farms to find lodging and get food, for we are here in a deserted place.” ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> But Jesus said to them, “You give them something to eat.” They replied, “We have no more than five loaves and two fish, unless we go and buy food for all these people.” ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> (For they were five thousand <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn757|†]]</sup> men). Jesus then said to his disciples, “Make them sit down in groups of about fifty each.” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> They did so, and made them all sit down. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> He took the five loaves and the two fish, and looking up to heaven, he blessed them, and broke them, and gave them to the disciples to set before the multitude. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Everybody ate, and all were satisfied. The disciples gathered up twelve baskets of broken pieces that were left over. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> Once, as Jesus <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn758|†]]</sup> was praying alone and the disciples were with him, he asked them, “Who do the multitudes say that I am?” ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> They answered, “‘John the Baptizer,’ but others say, ‘Elias (Elijah),’ and others, that one of the old prophets is risen again.” ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Jesus said to them, “But who do you say that I am?” Peter answered, “The Christ of God!” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> But he warned them and commanded them to tell this to no one, ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> saying, “The Son of Man must suffer many things, be rejected by the presbyters, the chief priests and the scribes, and be killed, and the third day be raised up.” ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Jesus said to all, “Anyone who desires to come after me must deny himself, take up his cross daily, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn759|†]]</sup> and follow me. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> For whoever desires to save his life will lose it, but whoever will lose his life for my sake will save it. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> Indeed what profit is there if someone gains the whole world but loses or surrenders <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn760|†]]</sup> his own self? ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> If anyone is ashamed of me and my words, the Son of Man will be ashamed of him when he comes in his glory, and in the glory of the Father and of the holy angels. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> But I tell you the truth: There are some who stand here who will in no way taste of death until they see the Kingdom of God.” ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> About eight days after these sayings, Jesus took with him Peter, John, and James, and went up to the mountain to pray. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> As he was praying, the appearance of his face was altered, and his clothing became white and dazzling. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And behold, two men were talking with him, who were Moses and Elias (Elijah). ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> They appeared in glory and spoke of his departure, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn761|†]]</sup> which he was about to accomplish in Jerusalem. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Now Peter and those who were with him were heavy with sleep, but when they were fully awake, they saw his glory and the two men who stood with him. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> As Moses and Elias (Elijah) <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn762|†]]</sup> were departing from him, Peter said to Jesus, “Master, it is good for us to be here! Let us make three tents: one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elias (Elijah).” He did not know what he was saying. ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> While Peter was saying these things, a cloud came and overshadowed them, and they were afraid as they entered into the cloud. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> A voice came out of the cloud, saying, “This is my beloved Son. Listen to him!” ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> And after the voice had spoken, Jesus was found alone. They kept silent and told no one in those days of what they had seen. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> On the next day, when they had come down from the mountain, a great multitude met him. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> A man from the crowd called out, saying, “Teacher, I beg you to look at my son, for he is my only child. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Behold, when a spirit seizes him, he suddenly cries out and it convulses him so that he foams [at the mouth]. It scarcely ever leaves him and is breaking him. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> I begged your disciples to cast it out, but they could not.” ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> Jesus answered, “Faithless and perverse generation, how long shall I be with you and bear with you? Bring your son here.” ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> While the boy was still coming, the demon threw him down and convulsed him violently. But Jesus rebuked the unclean spirit, healed the boy, and gave him back to his father. ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> And all were astonished at the majesty of God. While everyone was marveling at all that Jesus did, he said to his disciples, ^v43

###### v44
<span class="vn">44</span> “Let these words sink into your ears, for the Son of Man will be delivered up into the hands of men.” ^v44

###### v45
<span class="vn">45</span> But they did not understand this saying; it was concealed from them so that they should not understand its meaning, and they were afraid to ask him about this saying. ^v45

###### v46
<span class="vn">46</span> A dispute sprang up among them about which [one] of them was the greatest. ^v46

###### v47
<span class="vn">47</span> Jesus, perceiving the reasoning of their hearts, took a little child and set him by his side. ^v47

###### v48
<span class="vn">48</span> He then said to them, “Whoever receives this little child in my Name receives me. Whoever receives me receives him who sent me. For whoever is the least among you all, this one is great.” ^v48

###### v49
<span class="vn">49</span> John said, “Master, we saw someone casting out demons in your Name, and we tried to stop <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn763|†]]</sup> him because he does not follow with us.” ^v49

###### v50
<span class="vn">50</span> Jesus said to him, “Do not forbid <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn764|†]]</sup> him, for he is not against us. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn765|†]]</sup> For whoever is not against us is for us.” <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn766|†]]</sup> ^v50

###### v51
<span class="vn">51</span> It came to pass, when the days were near for him to be taken up, that he resolutely set his face <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn767|†]]</sup> to go to Jerusalem, ^v51

###### v52
<span class="vn">52</span> and he sent messengers ahead of him. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn768|†]]</sup> They went and entered into a village of the Samaritans in order to make preparation for him. ^v52

###### v53
<span class="vn">53</span> But the people did not receive him because he was heading for Jerusalem. ^v53

###### v54
<span class="vn">54</span> When his disciples James and John saw this, they said, “Lord, do you want us to command fire to come down from heaven and destroy <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn769|†]]</sup> them, just as Elias (Elijah) did?” ^v54

###### v55
<span class="vn">55</span> But Jesus turned around and rebuked them: “You do not know of what kind of spirit you are. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — EOB Notes#^edn770|†]]</sup> ^v55

###### v56
<span class="vn">56</span> The Son of Man did not come to destroy people’s lives but to save them.” And they went on to another village. ^v56

###### v57
<span class="vn">57</span> Now it happened that as they went on the way, a certain man said to him, “I want to follow you wherever you go, Lord!” ^v57

###### v58
<span class="vn">58</span> Jesus said to him, “The foxes have holes, and the birds of heaven have nests, but the Son of Man has no place to lay his head.” ^v58

###### v59
<span class="vn">59</span> He said to another, “Follow me!” But the man said, “Lord, allow me first to go and bury my father.” ^v59

###### v60
<span class="vn">60</span> But Jesus replied, “Leave the dead to bury their own dead. But you go and announce the Kingdom of God.” ^v60

###### v61
<span class="vn">61</span> Another also said, “I want to follow you, Lord, but first allow me to say goodbye to those who are at my house!” ^v61

###### v62
<span class="vn">62</span> But Jesus said to him, “No one who has put his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the Kingdom of God.” ^v62

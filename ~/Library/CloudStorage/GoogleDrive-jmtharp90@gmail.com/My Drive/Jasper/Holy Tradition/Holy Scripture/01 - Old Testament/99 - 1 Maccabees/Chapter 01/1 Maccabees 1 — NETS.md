###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And it happened after the triumph of Alexander the Macedonian the son of Philip, who came out of the land of Chettiim and defeated Darius, king of the Persians and Medes, and became king in his place, formerly being king of Greece— ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> and he conducted many wars and seized fortresses and slaughtered the kings of the earth. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And he penetrated to the ends of the earth and took the spoils of a multitude of nations. And the earth became quiet before him, and he was exalted, and his heart was uplifted. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And he gathered a very powerful force and ruled over countries, nations and tyrants, and they became tributary to him. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And after this he fell upon his bed and knew that he was dying. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And he called his distinguished servants, who had been raised with him from their youth, and divided his kingdom among them while he was still living. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> And Alexander ruled for twelve years and died. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> And his servants took control, each in his own place. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And they all put on crowns after he died, as did their sons after them for many years, and they multiplied evils on the earth. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And out of them arose a sinful root, Antiochus Epiphanes son of Antiochus the king, who had been a hostage in Rome and became king in the one hundred thirty-seventh year of the kingdom of the Greeks. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> In those days out of Israel came sons, transgressors of the law, and persuaded many, saying, “Let us go and make a covenant with the nations around us, because from the time we separated ourselves from them many evils have found us.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And the proposition seemed good in their eyes; ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> so some of the people took courage and went to the king, and he gave them the authority to follow the statutes of the nations. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> And they built a gymnasium in Hierosolyma according to the precepts of the nations, ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> and they fashioned foreskins for themselves and apostatized from the holy covenant and joined themselves to the nations and sold themselves to do evil. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> And the kingdom was ready in the sight of Antiochus, and he undertook to become king of the land of Egypt so that he might rule over the two kingdoms. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And he invaded Egypt with a heavily armed host, with chariots and elephants and a large fleet. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And he made war against Ptolemy, king of Egypt, and Ptolemy turned from before him and fled, and many fell wounded. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And they seized the fortified cities in the land of Egypt, and he took the spoils of the land of Egypt. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> And Antiochus returned after conquering Egypt in the one hundred forty-third year and went up against Israel and went up against Hierosolyma with a heavily armed host. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> And he entered the holy precinct with arrogance and took the golden altar and the lamp stand for the light and all its utensils ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> and the table of presentation and the libation bowls and the saucers and the golden censers and the veil and the crowns, and he stripped off all the gold ornamentation on the façade of the shrine. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And he took the silver and the gold and the precious vessels, and he took the hidden treasures, which he found. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And taking everything, he left for his own land and carried out murder and spoke with great arrogance. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And there was great mourning in Israel, in every place of theirs. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> And rulers and elders groaned, virgins and young men weakened, and the beauty of the women was deformed. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> Every bridegroom took up lamentation; she who sat in the bridal chamber was in mourning. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And the land quaked for those dwelling in it, and all the house of Iakob was clothed with shame. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> Two years later the king sent a chief revenue agent to the cities of Iouda, and he came to Ierousalem with a heavily-armed host. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And he spoke peaceful words to them with deceit, and they trusted him. But he fell upon the city suddenly and struck it a great blow, and he destroyed many people from Israel. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> And he took the spoils of the city and burned it with fire and tore down its houses and the walls around it. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> And they took the women and the children captive and took possession of the livestock. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And they reconstructed the city of Dauid with a large and fortified wall with fortified towers, and it became a citadel for them. ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And they stationed a sinful nation there, lawless men, and they became strong in it. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> And they stockpiled weapons and food, and gathering the plunder of Ierousalem, they stored it there and became a great threat. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> It became an ambush for the holy precinct and an evil enemy to Israel continuously. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> And they poured out innocent blood around the holy precinct and defiled the sanctuary. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> And the residents of Ierousalem left because of them, and it became a residence for foreigners and became foreign to those born of her, and her children abandoned her. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Her holy precinct became deserted as a wilderness; her feasts were turned to mourning, her sabbaths to disgrace, her honor to scorn. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> Her dishonor was multiplied to equal her former glory, and her exaltation was turned to mourning. ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> And the king wrote to all his kingdom for all to be as one people ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> and for each to abandon his own precepts; all the nations complied with the dictum of the king. ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> And many also from Israel approved of his service and sacrificed to idols and profaned the sabbath. ^v43

###### v44
<span class="vn">44</span> And the king sent documents carried by the hand of messengers to Ierousalem and the cities of Iouda for them to follow precepts foreign to the land ^v44

###### v45
<span class="vn">45</span> and to withhold whole burnt offerings and sacrifice and libation from the holy precinct and to profane sabbaths and feasts ^v45

###### v46
<span class="vn">46</span> and to defile holy precinct and holy ones, ^v46

###### v47
<span class="vn">47</span> to build altarsb and sacred precincts and houses to idols and to sacrifice swine and common animals ^v47

###### v48
<span class="vn">48</span> and to leave their sons uncircumcised, to make their souls abominable in every unclean and profane thing, ^v48

###### v49
<span class="vn">49</span> so as to forget the law and to change all the statutes. ^v49

###### v50
<span class="vn">50</span> And whoever would not abide by the command of the king would die. ^v50

###### v51
<span class="vn">51</span> According to all these words he wrote to all his kingdom and appointed supervisors over all the people and commanded all the cities of Iouda to sacrifice city by city. ^v51

###### v52
<span class="vn">52</span> And many of the people joined them, everyone who abandoned the law, and they did evil in the land. ^v52

###### v53
<span class="vn">53</span> And they forced Israel into hiding places, into every one of their places of refuge. ^v53

###### v54
<span class="vn">54</span> And on the fifteenth day of Chaseleu in the one hundred and forty-fifth year, he constructed an abomination of desolation on the altar, and in the cities around Iouda they built altars ^v54

###### v55
<span class="vn">55</span> and burned incense at the doors of the houses and in the city squares. ^v55

###### v56
<span class="vn">56</span> And the books of the law which they found they tore up and burned with fire. ^v56

###### v57
<span class="vn">57</span> And wherever there was found in someone’s possession a book of the covenant, or if someone was conforming to the law, the judgment of the king put them to death. ^v57

###### v58
<span class="vn">58</span> And they were using their power on those who were found in Israel month by month in the cities. ^v58

###### v59
<span class="vn">59</span> On the twenty-fifth of the month they were sacrificing on the altar that was on top of the sacrificial altar. ^v59

###### v60
<span class="vn">60</span> And the women who had circumcised their children they put to death according to the ordinance, ^v60

###### v61
<span class="vn">61</span> and they hung the babies from their necks and put to death their families and those who circumcised them. ^v61

###### v62
<span class="vn">62</span> But many in Israel remained strong and fortified themselves not to eat common things. ^v62

###### v63
<span class="vn">63</span> And they preferred to die so as not to be contaminated by food and not to defile the holy covenant, and they died. ^v63

###### v64
<span class="vn">64</span> And there was a very great wrath upon Israel. ^v64

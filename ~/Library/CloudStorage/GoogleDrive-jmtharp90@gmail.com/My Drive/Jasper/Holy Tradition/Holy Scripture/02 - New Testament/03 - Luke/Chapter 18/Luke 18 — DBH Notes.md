### [[Luke 18#v25|18:25]]
^v25
> [!tn] 18:25 ^fn342
> The text speaks of a κάμηλος (*kamēlos,* acc. *kamēlon*), “camel,” but from the early centuries it has been an open question whether it should really be the homophonous (but poorly attested) word κάμιλος (*kamilos*), “rope,” “hawser”: a more symmetrical but less piquant analogy.

### [[Luke 18#v26|18:26]]
^v26
> [!tn] 18:26 ^fn341
> καὶ τίς δύναται σωθῆναι (*kai tis dynatai sōthēnai*): often translated as “Who then can be saved?” or “Can anyone then be saved?” but I take the import (specifically as regards the τίς) to be “Can any [rich man] then be saved?”

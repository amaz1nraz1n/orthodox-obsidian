*The Believer’s Relationship to the Holy Spirit*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus. <sup class="nt-tc">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450081|‡]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> For the law of the life-giving Spirit <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450082|*]]</sup> in Christ Jesus has set you <sup class="nt-tc">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450083|‡]]</sup> free from the law of sin and death. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> For God achieved what the law could not do because <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450084|*]]</sup> it was weakened through the flesh. By sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh and concerning sin, he condemned sin in the flesh, ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> so that the righteous requirement of the law may be fulfilled in us, who do not walk according to the flesh but according to the Spirit. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> For those who live according to the flesh have their outlook shaped by <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450085|*]]</sup> the things of the flesh, but those who live according to the Spirit have their outlook shaped by the things of the Spirit. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> For the outlook <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450086|*]]</sup> of the flesh is death, but the outlook of the Spirit is life and peace, ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> because the outlook of the flesh is hostile to God, for it does not submit to the law of God, nor is it able to do so. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Those who are in the flesh cannot please God. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> You, however, are not in <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450087|*]]</sup> the flesh but in the Spirit, if indeed the Spirit of God lives in you. Now if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, this person does not belong to him. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> But if Christ is in you, your body is dead because of sin, but <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450088|*]]</sup> the Spirit is your life <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n450089|*]]</sup> because of righteousness. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> Moreover if the Spirit of the one <sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500810|†]]</sup> who raised Jesus from the dead lives in you, the one who raised Christ <sup class="nt-tc">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500811|‡]]</sup> from the dead will also make your mortal bodies alive through his Spirit who lives in you. <sup class="nt-tc">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500812|‡]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> So then, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500813|*]]</sup> brothers and sisters, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500814|*]]</sup> we are under obligation, not to the flesh, to live according to the flesh ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> (for if you live according to the flesh, you will <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500815|*]]</sup> die), <sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500816|†]]</sup> but if by the Spirit you put to death the deeds of the body you will live. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> For all who are led by the Spirit of God are <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500817|*]]</sup> the sons of God. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> For you did not receive the spirit of slavery leading again to fear, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500818|*]]</sup> but you received the Spirit of adoption, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500819|*]]</sup> by whom <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500820|*]]</sup> we cry, “ Abba, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500821|*]]</sup> Father.” ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> The Spirit himself bears witness to <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500822|*]]</sup> our spirit that we are God’s children. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And if children, then heirs (namely, heirs of God and also fellow heirs with Christ) <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500823|*]]</sup> —if indeed we suffer with him so we may also be glorified with him. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> For I consider that our present sufferings cannot even be compared <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500824|*]]</sup> to the coming glory that will be revealed to us. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> For the creation eagerly waits for the revelation of the sons of God. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For the creation was subjected to futility—not willingly but because of God <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500825|*]]</sup> who subjected it—in hope ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> that the creation itself will also be set free from the bondage of decay into the glorious freedom of God’s children. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> For we know that the whole creation groans and suffers together until now. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Not only this, but we ourselves also, who have the firstfruits of the Spirit, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500826|*]]</sup> groan inwardly as we eagerly await our adoption, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500827|*]]</sup> the redemption of our bodies. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500828|*]]</sup> ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> For in hope we were saved. Now hope that is seen is not hope, because who hopes for what he sees? ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> But if we hope for what we do not see, we eagerly wait for it with endurance. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500829|*]]</sup> ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> In the same way, the Spirit helps us in our weakness, for we do not know how we should pray, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500830|*]]</sup> but the Spirit himself intercedes for us with inexpressible groanings. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> And he <sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500831|†]]</sup> who searches our hearts knows the mind of the Spirit, because the Spirit <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500832|*]]</sup> intercedes on behalf of the saints according to God’s will. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And we know that all things work together <sup class="nt-tc">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500833|‡]]</sup> for good for those who love God, who are called according to his purpose, ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> because those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son, that his Son <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500834|*]]</sup> would be the firstborn among many brothers and sisters. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500835|*]]</sup> ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And those he predestined, he also called; and those he called, he also justified; and those he justified, he also glorified. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> What then shall we say about these things? If God is for us, who can be against us? ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Indeed, he who <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500836|*]]</sup> did not spare his own Son, but gave him up for us all—how will he not also, along with him, freely give us all things? ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Who will bring any charge against God’s elect? <sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500837|†]]</sup> It is God who justifies. ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> Who is the one who will condemn? Christ <sup class="nt-tc">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500838|‡]]</sup> is the one who died (and more than that, he was raised), who is at the right hand of God, and who also is interceding for us. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> Who will separate us from the love of Christ? Will trouble, or distress, or persecution, or famine, or nakedness, or danger, or sword? <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500839|*]]</sup> ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> As it is written, “ For your sake we encounter death all day long; we were considered as sheep to be slaughtered.” <sup class="nt-fn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500840|†]]</sup> ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> No, in all these things we have complete victory <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500841|*]]</sup> through him <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500842|*]]</sup> who loved us! ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> For I am convinced that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor heavenly rulers, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Romans 8 — NET Notes#^n4500843|*]]</sup> nor things that are present, nor things to come, nor powers, ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> nor height, nor depth, nor anything else in creation will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord. ^v39

###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> In the origin there was the Logos, and the Logos was present with GOD,<sup class="nt-fn">[[John 1 — DBH Notes#^fn339|†]]</sup> and the Logos was god; ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> This one was present with GOD in the origin. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> All things came to be through him, and without him came to be not a single thing that has come to be. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> In him was life, and this life was the light of men. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And the light shines in the darkness, and the darkness did not conquer it. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> There came a man, sent by GOD, whose name was John; ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> This man came in witness, that he might testify about the light, so that through him all might have faith— ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> But only that he might testify about the light; he was not that light. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> It was the true light, which illuminates everyone, that was coming into the cosmos. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> He was in the cosmos, and through him the cosmos came to be, and the cosmos did not recognize him. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> He came to those things that were his own, and they who were his own did not accept him. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> But as many as did accept him, to them he gave the power to become GOD’s children—to those having faith in his name, ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Those born not from blood, nor from a man’s desire, but of GOD. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> And the Logos became flesh and pitched a tent among us, and we saw his glory, glory as of the Father’s only one, full of grace and truth. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> John testifies concerning him and has cried out, saying, “This was he of whom I said, ‘He who is coming after me has surpassed me, for he was before me.’” ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> For we all have received from his fullness, and grace upon grace; ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Because the Law was given through Moses, the grace and the truth came through Jesus the Anointed. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> No one has ever seen GOD; the one who is uniquely god, who is in the Father’s breast, that one has declared him. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And this is John’s testimony when the Judaeans of Jerusalem sent priests and Levites to him, so that they might ask him, “Who are you?” ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> And he avowed, and did not deny, and confessed that: “I am not the Anointed.” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> And they asked him, “What then? Are you Elijah?” And he says, “I am not.” “Are you the Prophet?”<sup class="nt-fn">[[John 1 — DBH Notes#^fn338|†]]</sup> And he answered, “No.” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> So they said to him, “Who are you? So that we may give an answer to those who have sent us, what do you say concerning yourself?” ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> He said, “I am a voice of one crying in the desert, ‘Make straight the way of the Lord,’ as Isaiah the prophet said.” ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And some who had been sent were of the Pharisees. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And they questioned him and said to him, “Why then do you baptize, if you are not the Anointed or Elijah or the Prophet?” ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> John answered them saying, “I baptize in water; among you there stands one whom you do not know, ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> The one coming after me, the thong of whose sandals I am not worthy to untie.” ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> These things occurred in Bethany beyond the Jordan where John was baptizing. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> The next day he sees Jesus coming toward him, and says, “See the lamb of God<sup class="nt-fn">[[John 1 — DBH Notes#^fn337|†]]</sup> who is taking away the sin of the cosmos. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> This is he concerning whom I have said, ‘A man is coming after me who has surpassed me, for he was before me.’ ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> And I myself did not recognize him, although I came baptizing in water so that he might be made manifest to Israel.” ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> John also testified by saying: “I have seen the Spirit descending as a dove from the sky, and he rested on him. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And I did not recognize him; rather he who sent me to baptize in water, that one said to me, ‘On whomever you see the Spirit descending, and resting upon him, this is he who baptizes in a Holy Spirit.’ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And I have seen and have borne witness that this man is the Son of God.” ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> The next day John again stood there, and two of his disciples as well. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> And, watching Jesus walking by, he says, “Look: the Lamb of God.” ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> And the two disciples heard him saying this and followed Jesus. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> And turning around, and seeing them following, Jesus says to them, “What do you seek?” And they said to him, “Rabbi”—which is to say, when translated, “Teacher”—“where are you staying?” ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> He says to them, “Come and you will see.” So they went and saw where he was staying, and stayed with him that day; it was about the tenth hour. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> One of the two men who had heeded John and were following him was Andrew, the brother of Simon Peter; ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> The first thing he does is find his own brother Simon and tell him, “We have found the Messiah” (which, being translated, is “the Anointed”). ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> He led him to Jesus. Looking at him, Jesus said, “You are Simon the son of John; you shall be called Cephas” (which is translated as “Peter” [Rock]). ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> On the following day he wished to go away into Galilee, and he finds Philip. And Jesus says to him, “Follow me.” ^v43

###### v44
<span class="vn">44</span> Now Philip was from Bethsaida, the city from which Andrew and Peter came. ^v44

###### v45
<span class="vn">45</span> Philip finds Nathanael and says to him, “We have found him of whom Moses wrote in the Law and the Prophets, Jesus son of Joseph, from Nazareth.” ^v45

###### v46
<span class="vn">46</span> And Nathanael said to him, “Can there be anything good out of Nazareth?” Philip says to him, “Come and see.” ^v46

###### v47
<span class="vn">47</span> Jesus saw Nathanael approaching him and says of him, “Look: truly an Israelite, in whom there is no guile.” ^v47

###### v48
<span class="vn">48</span> Nathanael says to him, “Where do you know me from?” Jesus answered and said to him, “Before Philip called you, I saw that you were beneath the fig tree.” ^v48

###### v49
<span class="vn">49</span> Nathanael answered him, “Rabbi, you are the Son of God, you are the king of Israel.” ^v49

###### v50
<span class="vn">50</span> Jesus replied and said to him, “You have faith because I told you I saw you below the fig tree? You shall see greater things than these.” ^v50

###### v51
<span class="vn">51</span> And he says to him, “Amen, amen, I tell you, you shall see the heavens open and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of Man.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[John 1 — DBH Notes#^fn336|†]]</sup> ^v51

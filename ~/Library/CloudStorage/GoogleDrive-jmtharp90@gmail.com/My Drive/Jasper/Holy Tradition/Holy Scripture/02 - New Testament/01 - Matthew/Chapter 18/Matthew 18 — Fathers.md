### [[Matthew 18#v3|Mt 18:3]]
^v3

> [!cite] John Chrysostom — Homily LXI on Matthew 18, 3, 4,5
> THE PARABLE OF THE WICKED SERVANT
>
> It is His purpose to alarm him by this threat, so He might bring him to 
> supplication, not that he should be sold ...Why then did He not do this, nor 
> forgive the debt before the account? He did it desiring to teach him from 
> how many obligations He is delivering him, that in this way at least he might 
> become more mild towards his fellow-servant ..He gave more than he 
> asked, remission and forgiveness of the entire debt ...But even so, He willed 
> that the other also seem to contribute something, that he might not be ex- 
> ceedingly covered with shame, and that he being schooled in his own 
> calamities, might be indulgent to his fellow servant ...Let us, the covetous, 
> listen well, for even to us is the word spoken. Let us hearken also, the 
> merciless, and the cruel, for not to others are we cruel, but to ourselves. 
> When then you are minded to be revengeful, consider that against yourself 
> are you revengeful, not against another, that you are binding up your own 
> sins, not your neighbor’s ...Now the sentence is of much indignation, and 
> vengeance and punishment ...Two things therefore He requires here, both 
> to condemn ourselves for our sins, and to forgive others ...and from the
>
> eart.
>
> St. John Chrysostom. Homily LXI on Matthew XVIII, 3, 4,5.

### [[Matthew 18#v4|Mt 18:4]]
^v4

> [!cite] John Chrysostom — Homily LIX on Matthew 18, 4, 5
> IN HEAVEN, THE LITTLE ONES’ ANGELS ALWAYS SEE THE FACE 
> OF MY FATHER
>
> He calls the little ones not those who are really little, but those who are 
> so esteemed by the multitude, the poor, the objects of contempt, the un- 
> known (for how should he be little who is equal to the value of the whole) 
> It is evident that the saints have angels, or even all men. For the apostle 
> too says of the women, ‘That she ought to have power on her head, because 
> of the angels’ (I Cor.11:10)...Do you see by how many things He is ur- 
> ging towards the care of our unfortunate brethren? ...Let us not then be 
> careless about such souls as these. For all these things are said for this ob- 
> ject.
>
> St. John Chrysostom. Homily LIX on Matthew XVIII, 4, 5.

> [!cite] John Chrysostom — Homily LXII on Matthew 19, 4
> UNLESS YOU ARE CONVERTED AND BECOME AS LITTLE 
> CHILDREN, YOU WILL BY NO MEANS ENTER THE KINGDOM OF 
> HEAVEN
>
> And why did the disciples repel the 
> little children? For dignity. What then 
> does He do? Teaching them to be lowly 
> and to trample underfoot worldly pride, 
> He receives them and takes them in His 
> arms, and to such as them promises the 
> kingdom ...For this is the limit of true 
> wisdom: to be simple with under- 
> standing. This is the angelic life; yes, for 
> the soul of a little child is pure from all 
> passions ...Though you show him the 
> queen with a diadem, he does not prefer her to his mother clad in rags ...for 
> he uses to distinguish what pertains to him and what is strange to him, not 
> its poverty or wealth, but love and friendship. And nothing more than 
> necessary things does he seck, but just to be satisfied from the breast ...For 
> since the Pharisees did what they did from craft and pride, therefore He 
> charges the disciples to be singlehearted.
>
> St. John Chrysostom. Homily LXII on Matthew XIX, 4.

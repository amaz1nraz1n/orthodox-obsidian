###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And having called the twelve together, he gave them power and authority, over all the demons and for curing diseases; ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And he sent them forth to proclaim the Kingdom of God and to heal diseases; ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And he said to them, “Take nothing for the road, neither staff, nor leather pouch, nor a loaf of bread, nor silver—nor have two tunics each. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And into whatever household you enter, stay there and venture out from there. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And whoever should not receive you, on leaving that city shake the dust from your feet as a testimony against them.” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And setting out they passed throughout the villages, everywhere proclaiming the good tidings and healing. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Now Herod the tetrarch heard of all the things taking place, and he was perplexed because it was said by some that John had been raised from the dead, ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> And by some that Elijah had appeared, and by others that one of the prophets of old had risen again. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> But Herod said, “I beheaded John; but who is this about whom I hear such things?” And he sought to see him. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And when they had returned the Apostles recounted to him the things they had done. And taking them aside he departed in private to a city called Bethsaida. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> But the crowds, knowing this, followed him; and, welcoming them, he spoke to them about the Kingdom of God, and he cured those who had need of healing. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And the day began to decline; and the twelve approached and said to him, “Send this crowd away so that, going to the surrounding villages and towns, they may find provisions, because here we are in a desert place.” ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> But he said to them, “You give them something to eat.” But they said, “We have no more than five loaves of bread and two fish among us, unless we go out to buy food for all these people.” ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> For there were about five thousand men. And he said to his disciples, “Make them recline in parties of about fifty each.” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And they did so and made everyone recline. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> And, taking the five loaves and the two fishes, looking up to heaven, he blessed and broke them; he gave them to the disciples to serve out to the crowd. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And everyone ate their fill; and they took up twelve baskets of the fragments left over. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And it happened that, as he was praying alone, the disciples were with him, and he questioned them, saying, “Who do the crowds say that I am?” ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And in reply they said, “John the Baptist; but others Elijah, and others that a certain prophet of old has risen again.” ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> And he said to them, “But you, who do you say that I am?” And in reply Peter said, “The Anointed of God.” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> But, admonishing them, he enjoined them to tell this to no one, ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Saying: “It is necessary that the Son of Man suffer many things and be rejected by the elders and chief priests and scribes and be killed, and on the third day be raised.” ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And he said to all, “If anyone wishes to come along after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross each day, and let him follow me. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> For whoever wishes to save his soul will lose it; but whosoever loses his soul for my sake, this one will save it. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> For what profit is there for a man gaining the whole cosmos but losing—or being deprived of—himself? ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> For whoever is ashamed of me and my words, of this one the Son of Man will be ashamed when he comes in his glory, the glory of the Father and of his holy angels. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> But I tell you truly, there are some of those standing in this place who most certainly would not taste of death until they see the Kingdom of God.” ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And it happened that, about eight days after these exchanges, he took Peter and John and James and went up into the mountain to pray. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> And as he was praying the appearance of his face became different and his raiment became gleaming white. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And look: Speaking with him were two men who were Moses and Elijah, ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> Who—appearing in glory—spoke to him about his journey forth, which he was about to complete in Jerusalem. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> And Peter and those with him were weighed down by sleep; but waking thoroughly they saw the glory of him and of the two men standing with him. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And it happened that, when they parted from him, Peter—not knowing what he is saying—said to Jesus, “Master, it is a good thing for us to be here, and let us make three tabernacles, one for you and one for Moses and one for Elijah.” ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And as he was saying these things a cloud came and overshadowed them; and as they entered into the cloud they were afraid. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> And a voice came out of the cloud, saying, “This is my Son, the chosen one, listen to him.” ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> And, when the voice had gone, Jesus was found alone, and they were silent, and during those days no one told any of the things they had seen. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> And the next day, as they came down from the mountain, it happened that a great crowd met him. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> And look: A man shouted out from the crowd, saying, “Teacher, I beg you to look upon my son, because he is my one and only child, ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> And look, a spirit takes hold of him and suddenly cries out and throws him about frothing and, bruising him, scarcely leaves him. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> And I implored your disciples that they might exorcize it, and they were unable.” ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> And in reply Jesus said, “O faithless and perverse generation, for how long shall I be with you and endure you? Bring your son here.” ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> But, while he was still approaching, the demon tore at him and convulsed him violently; but Jesus rebuked the impure spirit and healed the boy and restored him to his father. ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> And all were utterly astonished at the greatness of God. ^v43

###### v44
<span class="vn">44</span> “Store these words deep in your ears; for the Son of Man is about to be delivered over into the hands of men.” ^v44

###### v45
<span class="vn">45</span> But they did not understand this statement, and it was veiled from them so that they could not comprehend it, and they were afraid to question him about this remark. ^v45

###### v46
<span class="vn">46</span> And a debate began among them, concerning who among them might be the greater. ^v46

###### v47
<span class="vn">47</span> And Jesus, perceiving the reasoning of their heart, took a little child and stood him beside himself, ^v47

###### v48
<span class="vn">48</span> And said to them, “Whoever receives this little child in my name receives me; and whoever receives me receives the one having sent me. For he who remains the lesser among all, this one is great.” ^v48

###### v49
<span class="vn">49</span> Then, speaking out, John said, “Master, we saw someone exorcizing demons in your name, and we tried to prevent him, because he does not follow along with us.” ^v49

###### v50
<span class="vn">50</span> And Jesus said to him, “Do not prevent him, for he who is not against you is for you.” ^v50

###### v51
<span class="vn">51</span> And it happened, as the days leading to his being taken up above were being completed, that he turned his face to the journey to Jerusalem, ^v51

###### v52
<span class="vn">52</span> And he sent out messengers before his face, and as they journeyed they entered a village of the Samaritans in order to prepare for him; ^v52

###### v53
<span class="vn">53</span> And they did not receive him, because his face was turned toward the journey to Jerusalem. ^v53

###### v54
<span class="vn">54</span> And the disciples James and John, seeing this, said, “Lord, do you wish that we should command fire to descend from heaven and to destroy them?” ^v54

###### v55
<span class="vn">55</span> But turning he rebuked them. ^v55

###### v56
<span class="vn">56</span> And they went to another village. ^v56

###### v57
<span class="vn">57</span> And, as they went along the road, someone said to him, “I will follow you wherever you go.” ^v57

###### v58
<span class="vn">58</span> And Jesus said to him, “The foxes have holes and the birds of the sky nests, but the Son of Man has nowhere he may lay his head.” ^v58

###### v59
<span class="vn">59</span> And he said to someone else, “Follow me.” And he said, “Allow me first to go bury my father.” ^v59

###### v60
<span class="vn">60</span> But he said to him, “Leave the dead to bury their own dead, but you go announce the Kingdom of God.” ^v60

###### v61
<span class="vn">61</span> And another also said, “I will follow you, Lord; but first permit me to bid farewell to those at my house.” But Jesus said, “No one putting his hand to the plow and then looking to the things behind him is fit for the Kingdom of God.” ^v61

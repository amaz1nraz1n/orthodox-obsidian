###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> James, a bondservant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the Twelve tribes which are in the Dispersion: Greetings! ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Consider it as all joy, my brethren, when you fall into various trials, ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> knowing that the testing of your faith produces endurance. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Let endurance have its full effect, so that you may be perfect and whole, lacking in nothing. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Yet if any of you lacks wisdom, let such a one ask of God who gives to all abundantly and without finding fault; and it will be given to him. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> But ask in faith, without any doubting, for whoever doubts is like a wave of the sea, driven by the wind and tossed. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Such a person should not expect to receive anything from the Lord. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> This is being double-minded, unstable in every way. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Let the brethren of humble circumstances glory in their high position. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> But the rich should rejoice in being made humble, because like the flower in the grass, they will pass away. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> Indeed, the sun arises with the scorching wind and withers the grass; its flower falls and the beauty of its appearance perishes. Likewise, the rich shall fade away in their pursuits. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Blessed is the one who endures temptation because after being approved, he will receive the crown of life which the Lord promised to those who love him. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> When temptations come, let no one say, “I am tempted by God,” because God cannot be tempted by evil and he himself tempts no one. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> But a person is tempted when drawn away and enticed by his own lust. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> Then the lust, when it has conceived, gives birth to sin; and sin, when it is full grown, results in death. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Do not be deceived, my beloved brethren! ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights with whom there can be no variation or shifting shadow. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> He brought us forth of his own will by the word of truth, so that we should be a kind of first fruits of his creatures. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> So then, my beloved brethren: be swift to hear, slow to speak, and slow to anger! <sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — EOB Notes#^edn2245|†]]</sup> ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Anger does not produce the righteousness of God. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Therefore, putting away all [moral] impurity and remains of wickedness, receive with humility the implanted word which is able to save your souls. <sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — EOB Notes#^edn2246|†]]</sup> ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Be doers of the word, not only hearers who delude themselves! ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Certainly, if anyone is a hearer of the word and not a doer, such a person is like someone looking at his natural face in a mirror. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Seeing himself and going away, he immediately forgets what kind of person he was. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> But whoever looks into the perfect law of freedom and perseveres [in it], not being a hearer who forgets but a doer of the work, this one will be blessed in doing so. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> If anyone among you considers himself to be religious <sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — EOB Notes#^edn2247|†]]</sup> but does not bridle his tongue, such a one deceives his heart and his religion is worthless. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> Religion that is pure and undefiled in the sight of our God and Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their afflictions and to keep oneself unstained by the world. ^v27

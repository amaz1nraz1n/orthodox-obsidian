*Introduction to the Sacrificial Regulations*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Then the Lord called to Moses and spoke to him <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030011|*]]</sup> from the Meeting Tent: <sup class="nt-fn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030012|†]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> “Speak to the Israelites and tell them, ‘When <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030013|*]]</sup> someone <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030014|*]]</sup> among you presents an offering <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030015|*]]</sup> to the Lord, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030016|*]]</sup> you <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030017|*]]</sup> must present your offering from the domesticated animals, either from the herd or from the flock. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030018|*]]</sup> ^v2

*Burnt-Offering Regulations: Animal from the Herd*
###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> “‘If his offering is a burnt offering <sup class="nt-fn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n030019|†]]</sup> from the herd he must present it as a flawless male; he must present it at the entrance <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300110|*]]</sup> of the Meeting Tent for its <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300111|*]]</sup> acceptance before the Lord. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> He must lay his hand on the head of the burnt offering, and it will be accepted for him to make atonement <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300112|*]]</sup> on his behalf. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Then the one presenting the offering <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300113|*]]</sup> must slaughter the bull <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300114|*]]</sup> before the Lord, and the sons of Aaron, the priests, must present the blood and splash <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300115|*]]</sup> the blood against the sides of the altar, which is at the entrance of the Meeting Tent. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Next, the one presenting the offering <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300116|*]]</sup> must skin the burnt offering and cut it into parts, ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> and the sons of Aaron, the priest, <sup class="nt-tc">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300117|‡]]</sup> must put fire on the altar and arrange wood on the fire. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Then the sons of Aaron, the priests, must arrange the parts with the head and the suet <sup class="nt-tc">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300118|‡]]</sup> on the wood that is in the fire on the altar. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300119|*]]</sup> ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Finally, the one presenting the offering <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300120|*]]</sup> must wash its entrails and its legs in water and the priest must offer all of it up in smoke on the altar <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300121|*]]</sup> —it is <sup class="nt-tc">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300122|‡]]</sup> a burnt offering, a gift <sup class="nt-fn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300123|†]]</sup> of a soothing aroma to the Lord. ^v9

*Animal from the Flock*
###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> “‘If his offering is from the flock for a burnt offering <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300124|*]]</sup> —from the sheep or the goats—he must present a flawless male, ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> and must slaughter it on the north side of the altar before the Lord, and the sons of Aaron, the priests, will splash its blood against the altar’s sides. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Next, the one presenting the offering <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300125|*]]</sup> must cut it into parts, with its head and its suet, and the priest must arrange them on the wood that is in the fire on the altar. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Then the one presenting the offering must wash the entrails and the legs in water, and the priest must present all of it and offer it up in smoke on the altar—it is a burnt offering, a gift of a soothing aroma to the Lord. ^v13

*Offering of Birds*
###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> “‘If his offering to the Lord is a burnt offering of birds, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300126|*]]</sup> he must present his offering from the turtledoves or from the young pigeons. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300127|*]]</sup> ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> The priest must present it at the altar, pinch off <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300128|*]]</sup> its head and offer the head <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300129|*]]</sup> up in smoke on the altar, and its blood must be drained out against the side of the altar. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Then the priest <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300130|*]]</sup> must remove its entrails by cutting off its tail feathers, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300131|*]]</sup> and throw them <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300132|*]]</sup> to the east side of the altar into the place of fatty ashes, ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> and tear it open by its wings without dividing it into two parts. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Leviticus 1 — NET Notes#^n0300133|*]]</sup> Finally, the priest must offer it up in smoke on the altar on the wood which is in the fire—it is a burnt offering, a gift of a soothing aroma to the Lord. ^v17

*Ahaz Receives a Sign*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> During <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230071|*]]</sup> the reign of Ahaz son of Jotham, son of Uzziah, king of Judah, King Rezin of Syria and King Pekah son of Remaliah of Israel marched up to Jerusalem to do battle, but they were unable to prevail against it. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230073|*]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> It was reported to the family <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230074|*]]</sup> of David, “Syria has allied with <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230075|*]]</sup> Ephraim.” They and their people were emotionally shaken, just as the trees of the forest shake before the wind. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230076|*]]</sup> ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> So the Lord told Isaiah, “Go out with your son Shear Jashub <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230077|*]]</sup> and meet Ahaz at the end of the conduit of the upper pool that is located on the road to the field where they wash and dry cloth. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230078|*]]</sup> ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Tell him, ‘Make sure you stay calm! <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n230079|*]]</sup> Don’t be afraid. Don’t be intimidated <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300710|*]]</sup> by these two stubs of smoking logs, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300711|†]]</sup> or by the raging anger of Rezin, Syria, and the son of Remaliah. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Syria has plotted with Ephraim and the son of Remaliah to bring about your demise. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300712|*]]</sup> ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> They say, “Let’s attack Judah, terrorize it, and conquer it. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300713|*]]</sup> Then we’ll set up the son of Tabeel as its king.” <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300714|*]]</sup> ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> For this reason the Sovereign Lord says: “‘It will not take place; it will not happen. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> For Syria’s leader is Damascus, and the leader of Damascus is Rezin. Within sixty-five years Ephraim will no longer exist as a nation. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300715|*]]</sup> ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Ephraim’s leader is Samaria, and Samaria’s leader is the son of Remaliah. If your faith does not remain firm, then you will not remain secure.’” <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300716|*]]</sup> ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> The Lord again spoke to Ahaz: ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> “Ask for a confirming sign from the Lord your God. You can even ask for something miraculous.” <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300717|*]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> But Ahaz responded, “I don’t want to ask; I don’t want to put the Lord to a test.” <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300718|*]]</sup> ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> So Isaiah replied, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300719|*]]</sup> “Pay attention, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300720|*]]</sup> family <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300721|*]]</sup> of David. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300722|†]]</sup> Do you consider it too insignificant to try the patience of men? Is that why you are also trying the patience of my God? ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> For this reason the Lord himself will give you a confirming sign. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300723|*]]</sup> Look, this <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300724|*]]</sup> young woman <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300725|*]]</sup> is about to conceive <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300726|*]]</sup> and will give birth to a son. You, young woman, will name him <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300727|*]]</sup> Immanuel. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300728|†]]</sup> ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> He will eat sour milk <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300729|*]]</sup> and honey, which will help him know how <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300730|*]]</sup> to reject evil and choose what is right. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Here is why this will be so: <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300731|*]]</sup> Before the child knows how to reject evil and choose what is right, the land <sup class="nt-fn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300732|†]]</sup> whose two kings you fear will be desolate. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300733|*]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> The Lord will bring on you, your people, and your father’s family a time <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300734|*]]</sup> unlike any since Ephraim departed from Judah—the king of Assyria!” <sup class="nt-fn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300735|†]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> At that time <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300736|*]]</sup> the Lord will whistle for flies from the distant streams of Egypt and for bees from the land of Assyria. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300737|†]]</sup> ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> All of them will come and make their home <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300738|*]]</sup> in the ravines between the cliffs and in the crevices of the cliffs, in all the thorn bushes, and in all the watering holes. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300739|*]]</sup> ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> At that time <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300740|*]]</sup> the Lord will use a razor hired from the banks of the Euphrates River, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300741|*]]</sup> the king of Assyria, to shave the hair off the head and private parts; <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300742|*]]</sup> it will also shave off the beard. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> At that time <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300743|*]]</sup> a man will keep alive a young cow from the herd and a couple of goats. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> From the abundance of milk they produce, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300744|*]]</sup> he will have sour milk for his meals. Indeed, everyone left in the heart of the land will eat sour milk and honey. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> At that time <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300745|*]]</sup> every place where there had been 1,000 vines worth 1,000 silver shekels will be overrun <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300746|*]]</sup> with thorns and briers. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> With bow and arrow <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300747|*]]</sup> people will hunt <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300748|*]]</sup> there, for the whole land will be covered <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300749|*]]</sup> with thorns and briers. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> They will stay away from all the hills that were cultivated for fear of the thorns and briers. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300750|*]]</sup> Cattle will graze there, and sheep will trample on them. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Isaiah 7 — NET Notes#^n2300751|*]]</sup> ^v25

### [[Nehemiah 1#v1|1:1]]
^v1
> [!note] 1:1 ^f1498
> **Nehemiah** means “comforted of God.” **Shushan** is Susa, a provincial capital in Persia, its most prominent city and winter residence of King Artaxerxes ([[Esther 1#v2|Est 1:2]]). **Chislev** is late November/early December. **The twentieth year** is that of the reign of Artaxerxes I, dating this account at 445 BC.

### [[Nehemiah 1#v2|1:2]]
^v2
> [!note] 1:2 ^f1499
> **Hanani**, a presumed blood brother of Nehemiah, is later made ruler of Jerusalem ([[Nehemiah 7#v2|7:2]]). He accompanies some **men from Judah** on an 800-mile journey to Persia to tell Nehemiah the status of (a) the survivors of **the captivity** now back in Judah and (b) the city of **Jerusalem**.

### [[Nehemiah 1#v3|1:3]]
^v3
> [!note] 1:3 ^f1500
> The returned exiles are in **great distress,** for the **wall** and **gates** of **Jerusalem **are destroyed. Addressing these two problems—the wall and the gates—is key to understanding the Book of Nehemiah.

### [[Nehemiah 1#v4|1:4]]
^v4
> [!note] 1:4 ^f1501
> Nehemiah, as would Christ ([[Matthew 23#v37|Mt 23:37]]), laments prayerfully for the people of Jerusalem, acknowledging they were guilty of sin and disobedience. Moreover, though a layman, he stands with the prophets in interceding with God for the sake of the chosen people and striving to renew the people's obedience to the Law. Throughout Scripture, **fasting and praying** go together. For Orthodox Christians, the Church offers additional services of worship and intercession during seasons of fasting, especially Lent and Advent.

### [[Nehemiah 1#v5|1:5-11]]
^v5
> [!note] 1:5-11 ^f1502
> **No indeed** is Nehemiah's way of saying, “I'm not putting up with the damaged state of the walls and gates of Jerusalem.” Nehemiah's prayer reflects his devotion to the words of Scripture. The phrase **great and awesome God** is from [[Deuteronomy 7#v21|Dt 7:21]]. Verses [[Nehemiah 1#v8|8]] and [[Nehemiah 1#v9|9]] quote [[Deuteronomy 30#v1|Dt 30:1–5]]. **This man** . . . **the king** is Artaxerxes I, under whom Nehemiah held the office of **cupbearer** (v. [[Nehemiah 1#v11|11]]). He it is who would grant Nehemiah's request to return to Jerusalem ([[Nehemiah 2#v6|2:6–8]]).

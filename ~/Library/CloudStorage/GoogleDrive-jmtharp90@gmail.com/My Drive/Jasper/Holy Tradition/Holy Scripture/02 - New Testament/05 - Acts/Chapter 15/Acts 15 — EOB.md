###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Then certain men came down from Judea {to Antioch} and taught the brethren, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1453|†]]</sup> “Unless you are circumcised according to the custom of Moses, you cannot be saved!” ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> As a result, Paul and Barnabas had no small dispute and controversy with them. Therefore, the brethren appointed Paul, Barnabas and some of their own to go up to Jerusalem to the apostles and presbyters regarding this question. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> Having been sent on their way by the Church, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1454|†]]</sup> they passed through Phoenicia and Samaria, relating the conversion of the Gentiles and causing great joy to all the brethren. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> When they arrived in Jerusalem, they were received by the Church, the apostles and the presbyters, and they reported all that God had accomplished with them <and that he had opened a door of faith to the Gentiles.> <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1455|†]]</sup> ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> However, some believers from the sect of the Pharisees arose and said, “It is necessary to circumcise them and to command them to keep the law of Moses!” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> The apostles and the presbyters gathered together to discuss this matter. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> When there had been much discussion, Peter stood up and said to them, “Brethren, you know that some time ago, God made a choice among us, that by my mouth the nations should hear the word of the Good News and believe. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> God, who knows the heart, bore witness to them by giving them the Holy Spirit, just as he had done to us. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> He made no distinction between us and them, purifying their hearts by faith. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And now, why do you put God to the test by putting a yoke on the neck of the disciples, one which neither our fathers nor we were able to bear? ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> But we believe that we are saved through the grace of the Lord Jesus, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1456|†]]</sup> just as they are.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> At this, the whole multitude kept silence. They listened to Barnabas and Paul report what signs and wonders God had accomplished among the nations through them. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> When they had finished, James said, “Brethren, listen to me! ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Simeon has reported how God first showed concern toward the Gentiles in order to take from them a people for his Name. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> This agrees with the words of the prophets. As it is written: ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> ‘After these things I will return.
 I will again build the tabernacle of David, which has fallen.
 I will again build its ruins.
 I will set it up, ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> So that the rest of the people may seek after the Lord;
 All the Gentiles who are called by my Name,’
 Says the Lord, who does all these things. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1457|†]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> All his works are known to God <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1458|†]]</sup> from eternity. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Therefore, I judge <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1459|†]]</sup> that we should not trouble those who turn to God from among the Gentiles. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Instead, we should write to them to abstain from the pollution of idols, from sexual immorality, from what is strangled, and from blood. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> For from generations of old, Moses has had in every city those who preach him, being read every Sabbath in the synagogues.” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Then it seemed good to the apostles and the presbyters, along with the whole Church, to choose some of their own men and to send them to Antioch with Paul and Barnabas: Judas (called Barsabbas) and Silas, [who were] leading men among the brethren. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Through their hands, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1460|†]]</sup> they wrote: ^v23

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> Accordingly, the men were sent off and arrived in Antioch where they gathered the community and delivered the letter. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> The people read it and rejoiced over the encouragement. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Judas and Silas (who were also prophets) encouraged the brethren with many words and strengthened them. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> After spending some time there, they were sent back with greetings from the brethren to those who had sent them out. ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> <But it seemed good to Silas to remain there>. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Acts 15 — EOB Notes#^edn1463|†]]</sup> ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> Paul and Barnabas stayed in Antioch, teaching and preaching the word of the Lord with many others. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> After some time, Paul said to Barnabas, “Let us revisit our brethren in every city where we proclaimed the word of the Lord, to see how they are doing!” ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> Barnabas was determined to take John (who was called Mark) along with them. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> However, Paul did not think that it was a good idea to take with them someone who had deserted them in Pamphylia and had not continued with them in the work. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Then the disagreement became so intense that they separated from each other. Barnabas took Mark with him and sailed away to Cyprus. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> But Paul chose Silas and having been entrusted by the brethren to the grace of God, they departed. ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> Paul went through Syria and Cilicia, strengthening the Churches. ^v41

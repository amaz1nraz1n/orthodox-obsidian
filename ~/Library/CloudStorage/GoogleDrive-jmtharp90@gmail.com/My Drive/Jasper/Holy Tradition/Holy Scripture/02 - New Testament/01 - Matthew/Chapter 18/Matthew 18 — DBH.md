###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> At that hour, the disciples approached Jesus, saying, “Who then is the greater in the Kingdom of the heavens?” ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And, calling a child forward, he stood the child in their midst, ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And said, “Amen, I tell you, unless you turn back and become as children, you most certainly may not enter into the Kingdom of the heavens. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> He therefore who will make himself small as this child, this one is the greater in the Kingdom of the heavens. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And whoever welcomes one such child in my name welcomes me; ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And, whoever causes one of these little ones who have faith in me to falter, it is better for him to have a millstone, of the kind turned by an ass, hung about his neck, and to be drowned in the depths of the sea. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Alas for the cosmos because of the occasions of faltering; for it is a necessity that occasions of faltering come about, but alas for the man through whom the occasion of faltering comes. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Now, if your hand or your foot causes you to falter, cut it off and fling it away from you; it is good for you to enter into life crippled or limping rather than, having two hands or two feet, to be cast into the fire of the Age. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And if your eye causes you to falter, tear it out and fling it away from you; it is good for you to enter into life with one eye rather than, having two eyes, to be cast into Hinnom’s Vale of fire. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> See to it that you are not contemptuous of one of these little ones; for I tell you that their angels in the heavens forever look upon the face of my Father in the heavens. [ ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> For the Son of Man has come to save the lost.] ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> How does it seem to you? If there is any man to whom a hundred sheep belong, and one of them wanders off, will he not leave the ninety-nine upon the hillside and go in search of the one that has strayed? ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And if he happens to find it, amen, I tell you that he takes more joy over it than over the ninety-nine who have not strayed. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> So it is not a desire that occurs to your Father in the heavens that one of these little ones should perish. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> Now, if your brother sins, go and remonstrate with him, between you and him privately. If he listens to you, you gain your brother; ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> If, though, he does not listen, take one or two others along with you, so that everything that is said may be confirmed by the mouths of two or three witnesses; ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> If, though, he refuses to listen to them, tell it to the assembly; and if he refuses to listen even to the assembly, let him be to you as the gentile or the tax-collector. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> Amen, I tell you, whatever things you bind on the earth will have been bound in heaven, and whatever things you unbind on the earth will have been unbound in heaven. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Again, [amen,] I tell you that if two among you agree on earth concerning everything they request, whatever it is, it shall come to pass for them, coming from my Father in the heavens. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For where there are two or three who have gathered in my name, I am there in their midst.” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Then Peter approached and said to him, “Lord, how many times will my brother sin against me and I shall forgive him? As many as seven times?” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Jesus says to him, “I tell you, not as many as seven times, but as many as seventy times seven. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Thus the Kingdom of the heavens has been likened to a man who was a king, who wished to reckon up accounts with his slaves. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And, as he began his reckoning, one who was indebted to him for ten thousand talents was brought forward. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And, as he was unable to make repayment, the master commanded he be sold, as well as his wife and children and all such things as he owns, and repayment be made. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Then the slave fell down and made obeisance to him, saying, ‘Be patient toward me, and I shall repay you everything.’ ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> And that slave’s master, being inwardly moved with compassion, released him and forgave him his loan. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> But going out that slave found one of his fellow slaves who owed him a hundred denarii, and seized hold of and throttled him, saying, ‘Pay me everything you owe.’ ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> Then his fellow slave fell down and implored him, saying, ‘Be patient toward me, and I shall repay you.’ ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> He would not, though, but went off and threw him into prison until he should repay the debt. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> Therefore, seeing the things that had taken place, his fellow slaves were extremely upset and went and explained to their master all the things that had happened. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Then, calling him forward, his master says to him, ‘You wicked slave, I forgave you all that debt after you implored me; ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Should you not also have mercy on your fellow slave, even as I had mercy on you?’ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And in anger his master delivered him to the inquisitors until he should repay everything owing to him. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> Thus also my heavenly Father will do to you unless, from your hearts, each one of you forgive his brother.” ^v35

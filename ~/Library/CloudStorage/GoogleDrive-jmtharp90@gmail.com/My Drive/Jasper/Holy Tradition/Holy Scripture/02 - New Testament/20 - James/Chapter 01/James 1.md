*Greeting*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> James, a bondservant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, To the twelve tribes which are scattered abroad: Greetings.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5898|†]]</sup> ^v1

*The Purpose of Trials*
###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> My brethren, count it all joy when you fall into various trials,<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5899|†]]</sup> ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> knowing that the testing of your faith produces patience. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> But let patience have its perfect work, that you may be perfect and complete, lacking nothing. ^v4

*Wisdom for Trials*
###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives to all liberally and without reproach, and it will be given to him.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5900|†]]</sup> ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> But let him ask in faith, with no doubting, for he who doubts is like a wave of the sea driven and tossed by the wind.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5901|†]]</sup> ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> For let not that man suppose that he will receive anything from the Lord; ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> he is a double-minded man, unstable in all his ways. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Let the lowly brother glory in his exaltation,<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5902|†]]</sup> ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> but the rich in his humiliation, because as a flower of the field he will pass away. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> For no sooner has the sun risen with a burning heat than it withers the grass; its flower falls, and its beautiful appearance perishes. So the rich man also will fade away in his pursuits. ^v11

*Temptations*
###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Blessed is the man who endures temptation; for when he has been approved, he will receive the crown of life which the Lord has promised to those who love Him.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5903|†]]</sup> ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Let no one say when he is tempted, “I am tempted by God”; for God cannot be tempted by evil, nor does He Himself tempt anyone.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5904|†]]</sup> ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> But each one is tempted when he is drawn away by his own desires and enticed.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5905|†]]</sup> ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> Then, when desire has conceived, it gives birth to sin; and sin, when it is full-grown, brings forth death. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Do not be deceived, my beloved brethren. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow of turning.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5906|†]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> Of His own will He brought us forth by the word of truth, that we might be a kind of firstfruits of His creatures.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5907|†]]</sup> ^v18

*The Corrective: Hear and Do*
###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> So then,<sup class="nt-tc">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^fvar690|‡]]</sup> my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath;<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5908|†]]</sup> ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> for the wrath of man does not produce the righteousness of God. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Therefore lay aside all filthiness and overflow of wickedness, and receive with meekness the implanted word, which is able to save your souls.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5909|†]]</sup> ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> But be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> For if anyone is a hearer of the word and not a doer, he is like a man observing his natural face in a mirror; ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> for he observes himself, goes away, and immediately forgets what kind of man he was. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> But he who looks into the perfect law of liberty and continues in it, and is not a forgetful hearer but a doer of the work, this one will be blessed in what he does.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5910|†]]</sup> ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> If anyone among you<sup class="nt-tc">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^fvar691|‡]]</sup> thinks he is religious, and does not bridle his tongue but deceives his own heart, this one's religion is useless.<sup class="nt-fn">[[James 1 — OSB Notes#^f5911|†]]</sup> ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> Pure and undefiled religion before God and the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their trouble, and to keep oneself unspotted from the world. ^v27

*Who Is the Greatest?*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> At that time the disciples came to Jesus, saying, “Who then is greatest in the kingdom of heaven?”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4258|†]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Then Jesus called a little child to Him, set him in the midst of them, ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> and said, “Assuredly, I say to you, unless you are converted and become as little children, you will by no means enter the kingdom of heaven. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Therefore whoever humbles himself as this little child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> Whoever receives one little child like this in My name receives Me. ^v5

*Scandals and Temptations*
###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> “Whoever causes one of these little ones who believe in Me to sin, it would be better for him if a millstone were hung around his neck, and he were drowned in the depth of the sea.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4259|†]]</sup> ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Woe to the world because of offenses! For offenses must come, but woe to that man by whom the offense comes! ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> “If your hand or foot causes you to sin, cut it off and cast it from you. It is better for you to enter into life lame or maimed, rather than having two hands or two feet, to be cast into the everlasting fire.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4260|†]]</sup> ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And if your eye causes you to sin, pluck it out and cast it from you. It is better for you to enter into life with one eye, rather than having two eyes, to be cast into hell fire. ^v9

*Finding the Lost Sheep*
###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> “Take heed that you do not despise one of these little ones, for I say to you that in heaven their angels always see the face of My Father who is in heaven.<sup class="nt-cit">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fcit51|¶]]</sup><sup class="nt-lit">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fx238|☩]]</sup> ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> For the Son of Man has come to save that which was lost.<sup class="nt-cross">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fcross28|§]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> “What do you think? If a man has a hundred sheep, and one of them goes astray, does he not leave the ninety-nine and go to the mountains to seek the one that is straying?<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4261|†]]</sup> ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And if he should find it, assuredly, I say to you, he rejoices more over that sheep than over the ninety-nine that did not go astray. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Even so it is not the will of your Father who is in heaven that one of these little ones should perish. ^v14

*Discipline in the Church*
###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> “Moreover if your brother sins against you, go and tell him his fault between you and him alone. If he hears you, you have gained your brother.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4262|†]]</sup> ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> But if he will not hear, take with you one or two more, that ‘by the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established.’<sup class="nt-cross">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fcross29|§]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And if he refuses to hear them, tell it to the church. But if he refuses even to hear the church, let him be to you like a heathen and a tax collector.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4263|†]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> “Assuredly, I say to you, whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4264|†]]</sup> ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> “Again I say<sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fvar63|‡]]</sup> to you that if two of you agree on earth concerning anything that they ask, it will be done for them by My Father in heaven. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For where two or three are gathered together in My name, I am there in the midst of them.” ^v20

*Forgiveness in the Church*
###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Then Peter came to Him and said, “Lord, how often shall my brother sin against me, and I forgive him? Up to seven times?”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4265|†]]</sup> ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Jesus said to him, “I do not say to you, up to seven times, but up to seventy times seven. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Therefore the kingdom of heaven is like a certain king who wanted to settle accounts with his servants. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And when he had begun to settle accounts, one was brought to him who owed him ten thousand talents. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> But as he was not able to pay, his master commanded that he be sold, with his wife and children and all that he had, and that payment be made.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^f4266|†]]</sup> ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> The servant therefore fell down before him, saying, ‘Master, have patience with me, and I will pay you all.’ ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> Then the master of that servant was moved with compassion, released him, and forgave him the debt. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> “But that servant went out and found one of his fellow servants who owed him a hundred denarii; and he laid hands on him and took him by the throat, saying, ‘Pay me what you owe!’ ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> So his fellow servant fell down at his feet<sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fvar64|‡]]</sup> and begged him, saying, ‘Have patience with me, and I will pay you all.’<sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fvar65|‡]]</sup> ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And he would not, but went and threw him into prison till he should pay the debt. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> So when his fellow servants saw what had been done, they were very grieved, and came and told their master all that had been done. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Then his master, after he had called him, said to him, ‘You wicked servant! I forgave you all that debt because you begged me. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Should you not also have had compassion on your fellow servant, just as I had pity on you?’ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And his master was angry, and delivered him to the torturers until he should pay all that was due to him. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> “So My heavenly Father also will do to you if each of you, from his heart, does not forgive his brother his trespasses.”<sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — OSB Notes#^fvar66|‡]]</sup> ^v35

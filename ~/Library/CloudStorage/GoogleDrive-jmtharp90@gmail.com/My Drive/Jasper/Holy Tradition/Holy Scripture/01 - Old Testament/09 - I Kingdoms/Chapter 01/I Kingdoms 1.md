*Elkanah at Shiloh*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> There was a certain man of Ramathaim Zophim, of the mountain of Ephraim, whose name was Elkanah the son of Jeremeel, the son of Elihu, the son of Toke in Nasiv Ephraim.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f931|†]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> He had two wives. The name of one was Hannah, and the name of the other was Peninnah. Peninnah had children, but Hannah none.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f932|†]]</sup> ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And each year the man went up from his city of Ramathaim to worship and sacrifice to Lord Sabaoth in Shiloh. Eli and his two sons, Hophni and Phinehas, both priests of the Lord, went there as well.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f933|†]]</sup> ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And on the day he came, Elkanah sacrificed and gave a portion to Peninnah his wife, and to his sons and daughters. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> To Hannah, he gave one portion, since she was without child. However Elkanah loved Hannah more than the other one, but the Lord had closed her womb. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Because the Lord did not give to her a child during her tribulation, and during the discouragement of her tribulation, she was broken-hearted. The Lord had closed her womb and did not give her a child. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> So year by year when she went up to the house of the Lord, she was broken-hearted and wept and did not eat.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f934|†]]</sup> ^v7

*Hannah's Vow*
###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> Then Elkanah, her husband, said to her, “Hannah.” And she said to him, “Here I am, lord.” Then he said to her, “What is the matter with you? Why do you not eat? And why are you heartsick? Am I not better for you than ten children?” ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> So Hannah arose after they had finished eating in Shiloh, and she sat down before the Lord. Eli the priest was sitting nearby on the seat by the doorpost of the temple of the Lord.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f935|†]]</sup> ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Hannah was grieved in spirit and prayed to the Lord, lamenting and weeping. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> She prayed a vow to the Lord God and said, “O Lord, my God of Sabaoth, if you are looking, look with favor upon the lowly state of Your handmaiden and remember me. I pray You, give Your handmaiden a seed of men, a son, and I will dedicate him before You as a gift until the day of his death. He will not drink wine nor intoxicating drink, and no razor shall come upon his head.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f936|†]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And as she kept praying before the Lord, Eli watched her mouth. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And as she spoke in her heart, only her lips moved, but her voice was not heard. Therefore Eli thought she was drunk.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f937|†]]</sup> ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> The servant of God said to her, “For how long will you remain drunk? Cast off your wine, and go out from the face of the Lord!” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> But Hannah answered and said, “No lord, I am a woman for whom this day is difficult. I drank neither wine nor intoxicating drink, but I pour out my soul before the Lord. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Do not consider your handmaiden a daughter of pestilence just because of the multitude of my idle words I have put forth.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f938|†]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Then Eli answered and said to her, “Go in peace, and may the God of Israel grant you the petition you asked of Him.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f939|†]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And she said, “Your handmaiden has found favor in your sight.” So the woman went her way and came into her lodging and ate and drank with her husband. Her face was no longer fallen.<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f940|†]]</sup> ^v18

*Birth and Dedication of Samuel*
###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Then they rose early in the morning and worshiped the Lord and went on their way back to Elkanah's house at Ramathaim. And he knew Hannah his wife, and the Lord remembered her. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> She conceived, and it came to be at the appointed time she bore a son, and she called him Samuel and said, “Because I asked for him from the Lord God Sabaoth.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f941|†]]</sup> ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Now the man Elkanah and all his house went up to Shiloh to offer the yearly sacrifice, his vow, and the tenth portion of all his land. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> But Hannah did not go up with him, for she said to her husband, “I will not go up until our boy is weaned from me. Then he will appear in the presence of the Lord and stay there continually.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f942|†]]</sup> ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And Elkanah her husband said to her, “Do what is good in your eyes; wait until you have weaned him. Only let the Lord confirm as true what was coming out of your mouth.” So the woman stayed and nursed her son until she weaned him. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And she went up with him to Shiloh with a calf three years old, loaves of bread, one ephah of flour, and a skin of wine. She came to the house of the Lord in Shiloh, and the child was with them. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And they came before the Lord, and his father slaughtered the offering he made yearly to the Lord. He brought the child and slaughtered the calf. Then Hannah, the mother of the young boy, went to Eli. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> And she said to him, “Lord, as your soul lives, I am the woman who was standing near you while I prayed to the Lord. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> For this child I prayed, and the Lord gave me my request which I asked from Him. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And I lend him to the Lord all the days which he lives to be fruitful to the Lord.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[I Kingdoms 1 — OSB Notes#^f943|†]]</sup> ^v28

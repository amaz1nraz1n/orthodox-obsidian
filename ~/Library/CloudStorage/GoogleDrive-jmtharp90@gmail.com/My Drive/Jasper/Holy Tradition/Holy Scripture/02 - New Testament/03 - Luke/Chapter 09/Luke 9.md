*The Twelve Sent Out*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Then He called His twelve disciples together and gave them power and authority over all demons, and to cure diseases.<sup class="nt-cit">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^fcit143|¶]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> He sent them to preach the kingdom of God and to heal the sick. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And He said to them, “Take nothing for the journey, neither staffs nor bag nor bread nor money; and do not have two tunics apiece. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> “Whatever house you enter, stay there, and from there depart. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And whoever will not receive you, when you go out of that city, shake off the very dust from your feet as a testimony against them.” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> So they departed and went through the towns, preaching the gospel and healing everywhere. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Now Herod the tetrarch heard of all that was done by Him; and he was perplexed, because it was said by some that John had risen from the dead,<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4539|†]]</sup> ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> and by some that Elijah had appeared, and by others that one of the old prophets had risen again. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Herod said, “John I have beheaded, but who is this of whom I hear such things?” So he sought to see Him. ^v9

*Feeding the Five Thousand*
###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And the apostles, when they had returned, told Him all that they had done. Then He took them and went aside privately into a deserted place belonging to the city called Bethsaida. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> But when the multitudes knew it, they followed Him; and He received them and spoke to them about the kingdom of God, and healed those who had need of healing.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4540|†]]</sup> ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> When the day began to wear away, the twelve came and said to Him, “Send the multitude away, that they may go into the surrounding towns and country, and lodge and get provisions; for we are in a deserted place here.” ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> But He said to them, “You give them something to eat.” And they said, “We have no more than five loaves and two fish, unless we go and buy food for all these people.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4541|†]]</sup> ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> For there were about five thousand men. Then He said to His disciples, “Make them sit down in groups of fifty.” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And they did so, and made them all sit down. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Then He took the five loaves and the two fish, and looking up to heaven, He blessed and broke them, and gave them to the disciples to set before the multitude.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4542|†]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> So they all ate and were filled, and twelve baskets of the leftover fragments were taken up by them. ^v17

*Peter's Confession: Jesus is the Christ*

<span class="vn">18</span> And it happened, as He was alone praying, that His disciples joined Him, and He asked them, saying, “Who do the crowds say that I am?”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4543|†]]</sup> ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> So they answered and said, “John the Baptist, but some say Elijah; and others say that one of the old prophets has risen again.” ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> He said to them, “But who do you say that I am?” Peter answered and said, “The Christ of God.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4544|†]]</sup> ^v20

###### v23

*The Transfiguration*
###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> Now it came to pass, about eight days after these sayings, that He took Peter, John, and James and went up on the mountain to pray.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4548|†]]</sup> ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> As He prayed, the appearance of His face was altered, and His robe became white and glistening. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And behold, two men talked with Him, who were Moses and Elijah, ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> who appeared in glory and spoke of His decease which He was about to accomplish at Jerusalem.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4549|†]]</sup> ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> But Peter and those with him were heavy with sleep; and when they were fully awake, they saw His glory and the two men who stood with Him. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Then it happened, as they were parting from Him, that Peter said to Jesus, “Master, it is good for us to be here; and let us make three tabernacles: one for You, one for Moses, and one for Elijah”—not knowing what he said. ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> While he was saying this, a cloud came and overshadowed them; and they were fearful as they entered the cloud. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> And a voice came out of the cloud, saying, “This is My beloved Son.<sup class="nt-tc">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^fvar232|‡]]</sup> Hear Him!” ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> When the voice had ceased, Jesus was found alone. But they kept quiet, and told no one in those days any of the things they had seen. ^v36

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> Suddenly a man from the multitude cried out, saying, “Teacher, I implore You, look on my son, for he is my only child. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> And behold, a spirit seizes him, and he suddenly cries out; it convulses him so that he foams at the mouth; and it departs from him with great difficulty, bruising him. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> So I implored Your disciples to cast it out, but they could not.”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 9 — OSB Notes#^f4550|†]]</sup> ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> Then Jesus answered and said, “O faithless and perverse generation, how long shall I be with you and bear with you? Bring your son here.” ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> And as he was still coming, the demon threw him down and convulsed him. Then Jesus rebuked the unclean spirit, healed the child, and gave him back to his father. ^v42

###### v45

###### v61

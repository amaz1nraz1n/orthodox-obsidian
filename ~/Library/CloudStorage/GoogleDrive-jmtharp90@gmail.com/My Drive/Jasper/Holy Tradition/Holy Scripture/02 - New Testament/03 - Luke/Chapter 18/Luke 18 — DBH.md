###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And he told them a parable on the necessity of their always praying and not becoming remiss, ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Saying, “In a certain city there was a certain judge who did not fear God and who had no concern for humankind. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And there was a widow in that city, and she came to him saying, ‘Grant me justice over against my adversary.’ ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And for some time he would not; but thereafter he said within himself, ‘Though indeed I do not fear God, nor do I have any concern for humankind, ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> I shall grant her justice simply because she bothers me, for fear that at the last she will entirely exhaust me with her visits.’” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And the Lord said, “Listen to what the unjust judge says; ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Will not God then surely bring about justice for his chosen ones crying to him day and night, and not delay over them for long? ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> I tell you, he will swiftly bring them justice. Yet, when the Son of Man comes, will he then find faith on the earth?” ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And he also spoke this parable to certain persons who were confident that they were upright while despising everyone else: ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> “Two men went up to the Temple to pray, the one a Pharisee, the other a tax-collector. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> The Pharisee stood up straight and prayed these things about himself: ‘God, I thank you that I am not like the rest of mankind—rapacious, unjust, adulterous—or even like this tax-collector; ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> I fast twice a week and tithe from everything whatsoever that I earn.’ ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> But the tax-collector, standing a good distance off, would not lift his eyes to heaven, but beat upon his breast, saying, ‘God, grant mercy to me, a sinner.’ ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> I tell you, the latter rather than the former went down to his house vindicated; because everyone who exalts himself will be humbled, and whoever humbles himself will be exalted.” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And they also brought babies to him, so that he might touch them; but seeing this the disciples admonished them. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> But Jesus called them forward, saying, “Allow the little children to come to me, and do not prevent them; for of such is the Kingdom of God. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Amen, I tell you, whoever does not receive the Kingdom of God as a little child shall most certainly not enter into it.” ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And a certain ruler inquired of him, “Good teacher, by doing what may I inherit life in the Age?” ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And Jesus said to him, “Why do you say I am good? No one is good except one—God. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> You know the commandments: Do not commit adultery, do not murder, do not steal, do not give false testimony, honor your father and mother.” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> And he said, “All of these I have kept from youth.” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> But hearing this Jesus said to him, “One thing is still lacking in you; sell everything, whatever you have, and distribute it to the destitute, and you will have a treasury in the heavens, and follow me.” ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And he, hearing these things, became very morose, for he was extremely rich. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And, seeing him, Jesus said, “How hard for those possessing wealth to enter the Kingdom of God, ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> For it is easier for a camel<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — DBH Notes#^fn342|†]]</sup> to enter through the eye of a needle than for a rich man to enter into the Kingdom of God.” ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> And those hearing this said, “Can any of them then be saved?”<sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — DBH Notes#^fn341|†]]</sup> ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> And he said, “Those things impossible for men are possible for God.” ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And Peter said to him, “Look: We have abandoned all that was ours and followed you.” ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> And he said to them, “Amen, I tell you that there is no one who has left home or wife or brothers or parents or children for the sake of the Kingdom of God, ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> Who does not surely receive many times as much in the present time and, in the Age to come, the life of that Age.” ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> And taking the Twelve aside he said to them, “Look: We are going up to Jerusalem, and everything written by the prophets concerning the Son of Man will be accomplished; ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> For he will be handed over to the gentiles and will be mocked and insulted and spat upon, ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And having flogged him they will kill him, and on the third day he will rise again.” ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And they understood none of this, and this message was hidden from them, and they did not know what they were being told. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> And it happened that, as he drew near to Jericho, a certain blind man was sitting beside the road begging. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> And hearing a crowd passing through he inquired what this might be. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> And they explained to him: “Jesus the Nazorean is going by.” ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> And he cried aloud, saying, “Jesus, son of David, have mercy on me.” ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> And those leading the way admonished him that he should be silent; but he cried out all the more, “Son of David, have mercy on me.” ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> And, coming to a standstill, Jesus ordered that he be brought to him. And, when he came near, he asked him, ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> “What do you wish I might do?” And he said, “Lord, that I might see again.” ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> And Jesus said to him, “See again; your faith has saved you.” ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> And on the instant he saw again, and followed him glorifying God. And all who saw this gave praise to God. ^v43

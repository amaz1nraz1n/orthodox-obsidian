*Questions About the Greatest*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> At that time the disciples came to Jesus saying, “Who is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven?” ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> He called a child, had him stand among them, ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> and said, “I tell you the truth, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400181|*]]</sup> unless you turn around and become like little children, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400182|†]]</sup> you will never <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400183|*]]</sup> enter the kingdom of heaven! ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Whoever then humbles himself like this little child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And whoever welcomes <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400184|*]]</sup> a child like this in my name welcomes me. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> “But if anyone causes one of these little ones who believe in me to sin, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400185|*]]</sup> it would be better for him to have a huge millstone <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400186|*]]</sup> hung around his neck and to be drowned in the open sea. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400187|*]]</sup> ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Woe to the world because of stumbling blocks! It <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400188|*]]</sup> is necessary that stumbling blocks come, but woe to the person through whom they come. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> If <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n400189|*]]</sup> your hand or your foot causes you to sin, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001810|†]]</sup> cut it off and throw it away. It is better for you to enter life crippled or lame than to have <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001811|*]]</sup> two hands or two feet and be thrown into eternal fire. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And if your eye causes you to sin, tear it out and throw it away. It is better for you to enter into life with one eye than to have <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001812|*]]</sup> two eyes and be thrown into fiery hell. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001813|*]]</sup> ^v9

*The Parable of the Lost Sheep*
###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> “See that you do not disdain one of these little ones. For I tell you that their angels in heaven always see the face of my Father in heaven. <sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001814|‡]]</sup> ^v10

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> What do you think? If someone <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001815|*]]</sup> owns a hundred <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001816|†]]</sup> sheep and one of them goes astray, will he not leave the ninety-nine on the mountains and go look for the one that went astray? <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001817|†]]</sup> ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And if he finds it, I tell you the truth, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001818|*]]</sup> he will rejoice more over it than over the ninety-nine that did not go astray. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> In the same way, your Father in heaven is not willing that one of these little ones be lost. ^v14

*Restoring Christian Relationships*
###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> “If <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001819|*]]</sup> your brother <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001820|*]]</sup> sins, <sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001821|‡]]</sup> go and show him his fault <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001822|*]]</sup> when the two of you are alone. If he listens to you, you have regained your brother. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> But if he does not listen, take one or two others with you, so that at the testimony of two or three witnesses every matter may be established. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001823|†]]</sup> ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> If <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001824|*]]</sup> he refuses to listen to them, tell it to the church. If <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001825|*]]</sup> he refuses to listen to the church, treat him like <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001826|*]]</sup> a Gentile <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001827|*]]</sup> or a tax collector. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001828|†]]</sup> ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> “I tell you the truth, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001829|*]]</sup> whatever you bind on earth will have been bound in heaven, and whatever you release on earth will have been released in heaven. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Again, I tell you the truth, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001830|*]]</sup> if two of you on earth agree about whatever you ask, my Father in heaven will do it for you. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001831|*]]</sup> ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For where two or three are assembled in my name, I am there among them.” ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Then Peter came to him and said, “Lord, how many times must I forgive my brother <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001832|*]]</sup> who sins against me? As many as seven times?” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Jesus said to him, “Not seven times, I tell you, but seventy-seven times! <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001833|*]]</sup> ^v22

*The Parable of the Unforgiving Slave*
###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> “For this reason, the kingdom of heaven is like a king who wanted to settle accounts with his slaves. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001834|*]]</sup> ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> As <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001835|*]]</sup> he began settling his accounts, a man who owed 10,000 talents <sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001836|†]]</sup> was brought to him. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> Because <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001837|*]]</sup> he was not able to repay it, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001838|*]]</sup> the lord ordered him to be sold, along with <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001839|*]]</sup> his wife, children, and whatever he possessed, and repayment to be made. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Then the slave threw himself to the ground <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001840|*]]</sup> before him, saying, <sup class="nt-tc">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001841|‡]]</sup> ‘Be patient with me, and I will repay you everything.’ ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> The lord had compassion on that slave and released him, and forgave him the debt. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> After <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001842|*]]</sup> he went out, that same slave found one of his fellow slaves who owed him 100 silver coins. <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001843|*]]</sup> So <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001844|*]]</sup> he grabbed him by the throat and started to choke him, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001845|*]]</sup> saying, ‘Pay back what you owe me!’ <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001846|*]]</sup> ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> Then his fellow slave threw himself down and begged him, <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001847|*]]</sup> ‘Be patient with me, and I will repay you.’ ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> But he refused. Instead, he went out and threw him in prison until he repaid the debt. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> When <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001848|*]]</sup> his fellow slaves saw what had happened, they were very upset and went and told their lord everything that had taken place. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Then his lord called the first slave <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001849|*]]</sup> and said to him, ‘Evil slave! I forgave you all that debt because you begged me! ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Should you not have shown mercy to your fellow slave, just as I showed it to you?’ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And in anger his lord turned him over to the prison guards to torture him <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001850|*]]</sup> until he repaid all he owed. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> So also my heavenly Father will do to you, if each of you does not forgive your <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001851|*]]</sup> brother <sup class="nt-tn">[[Matthew 18 — NET Notes#^n4001852|*]]</sup> from your heart.” ^v35

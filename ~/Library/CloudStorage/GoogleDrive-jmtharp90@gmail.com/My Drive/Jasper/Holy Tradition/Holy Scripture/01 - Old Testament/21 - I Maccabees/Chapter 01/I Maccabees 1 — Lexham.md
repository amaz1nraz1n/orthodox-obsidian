###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And it happened after the victory of Alexander, the son of Philip, the Macedonian, who went out from the land of the Hittites and struck down Darius, king of the Persians and Medes, and ruled in place of him—earlier he ruled over Greece. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And he participated in many battles and conquered strongholds and killed kings. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And he passed through as far as the farthest point of the earth and took spoils from many nations. And the land was silent before him, and his heart was elated and stirred up. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And he gathered a very mighty force and ruled over the territories, and nations, and tyrants,<sup class="nt-tn">[[I Maccabees 1 — Lexham Notes#^v4|*]]</sup> and they became to him as tribute. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And after this, he fell on his bed and perceived that he was going to die. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And he summoned his esteemed servants who raised him from childhood and divided for them his kingdom while he was living. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> And Alexander ruled twelve years and then died. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> And his subordinates ruled, each in his place. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And all of them put on crowns after his death, and their sons after them for many years. And they increased the amount of evil in the earth. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> And there came forth from them a sinful root: Antiochus Epiphanes, son of Antiochus, the king, who was a hostage in Rome. And he ruled in the one hundred and thirty-seventh year of the kingdom of the Greeks.<sup class="nt-tn">[[I Maccabees 1 — Lexham Notes#^v10|*]]</sup> ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> In those days, the lawless son went out of Israel, and they persuaded many, saying, “We should go and make a treaty with the nations around us, because from that time we separated from them, much evil has found us.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And the idea was attractive in their eyes. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And some from the people were willing and went to the king, and they gave them authority to perform the duties of the nations. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> And they built a gymnasium in Jerusalem according to the customs of the nations. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And they made a foreskin for themselves and discarded the holy covenant and joined with the Gentiles and were sold to act wickedly. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> And the kingdom was prepared for Antiochus. And he decided to exercise his rule over the land of Egypt so that he might rule over the two kingdoms. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> And he went into Egypt in fierce force, with chariots and elephants and with cavalry and with a great navy. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> And he prepared for war against Ptolemy, the king of Egypt. And Ptolemy was shamed from his face and fled. And many casualties fell. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And they captured the fortified city in the land of Egypt and took the spoils of the land of Egypt. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> And Antiochus turned after striking Egypt in the one hundred and forty-third year, and he went up against Israel and Jerusalem with a fierce army. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> And he entered the sanctuary in arrogance and took the gold altar and the lampstand for the light, and all its accessories, ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> and the table of presentation and the drink offering cups and the bowls and the golden censers and the veil and the crowns and the golden adornment applied to the face of the temple walls, and he peeled it all off. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> And he took the gold and silver and the valuable accessories. And he took the hidden treasures that he found. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> And having taken everything, he departed to his land. And he made a massacre and spoke with great arrogance. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> And there was great mourning in Israel, in all their places. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> And the rulers and elders groaned; virgins and young men lost their strength, and the beauty of women changed. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> Every bridegroom took up a lament; she who stayed in the bridal chamber was in mourning. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> And the land shook, for the inhabitants in it and the whole house of Jacob wore shame. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> After two years of time, the king sent a chief of tribute collection to the cities of Judah. And he came to Jerusalem with fierce force. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And he spoke to them peaceful words with cunning. And they trusted him. But he fell upon the city suddenly and struck them with a great slaughter and destroyed many people from Israel. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> And he took the spoils of the city and burned it with fire and broke down its buildings and its surrounding wall. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> And he took captive the women and children and took possession of the livestock. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And they built in the city of David a great wall and fortified towers, and it became for them as a fortress. ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> And they established there a sinful people, wicked men, and they became strong in it. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> And they stored weapons and provisions and, gathering the spoils of Jerusalem, they put it away there. And he became a great threat. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> And it became as an ambush against the sanctuary and as an evil menace to Israel through all time. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> And they shed innocent blood all around the sanctuary and defiled the sanctuary. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> And the inhabitants of Jerusalem fled because of them, and it became a settlement of foreigners, and it became foreign to the ones born in it, and its children abandoned it. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Her sanctuary was stripped bare like the wilderness; her feasts turned to grief, her Sabbaths to insult, her honor to contempt. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> According to her glory, her dishonor was multiplied, and her majesty turned to grief. ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> And the king wrote to his whole kingdom that all should be as one people, ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> and each should abandon his customs. And all the peoples complied according to the word of the king. ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> And many from Israel consented to his service, and they sacrificed to idols and defiled the Sabbath. ^v43

###### v44
<span class="vn">44</span> And the king sent documents in the hands of messengers to Jerusalem and the cities of Judah, going after the customs of foreigners of the land, ^v44

###### v45
<span class="vn">45</span> and to withhold burnt offerings and sacrifice and drink offering from the sanctuary and to profane Sabbaths and festivals, ^v45

###### v46
<span class="vn">46</span> and to defile the sanctuary and holy things, ^v46

###### v47
<span class="vn">47</span> to build altars and shrines and idols and sacrifice pigs and common animals, ^v47

###### v48
<span class="vn">48</span> and to leave their sons uncircumcised, to make repulsive their souls with all impurity and profanation ^v48

###### v49
<span class="vn">49</span> so as to forget the law and to change all their duties. ^v49

###### v50
<span class="vn">50</span> And whoever should not act according to the word of the king would die. ^v50

###### v51
<span class="vn">51</span> According to all these words, he wrote to his kingdom, and he appointed supervisors over all the people and ordered the cities of Judah to sacrifice according to city and city. ^v51

###### v52
<span class="vn">52</span> And many assembled from the people against them—namely, all who abandoned the law and did evil in the land ^v52

###### v53
<span class="vn">53</span> and put Israel in secret places, and in all kinds of hiding places, for their refuge. ^v53

###### v54
<span class="vn">54</span> And on the fifteenth day of Chislev, on the forty-fifth year, they built an abomination of desolation on the altar, and in the cities around Judah, they built altars. ^v54

###### v55
<span class="vn">55</span> And at the windows of their houses and in the streets, they burned incense. ^v55

###### v56
<span class="vn">56</span> And the documents of the law that they found, they burned with fire, after cutting them up. ^v56

###### v57
<span class="vn">57</span> And where the book of the covenant was found with someone, and if that person should give approval to the law, the decree of the king was, “Execute him.” ^v57

###### v58
<span class="vn">58</span> By their strength they did thus in Israel to the ones who were found during every month and month in the cities. ^v58

###### v59
<span class="vn">59</span> And on the fifth and twentieth of the month, they were sacrificing on the altar that was on the altar for burnt sacrifices. ^v59

###### v60
<span class="vn">60</span> And the women who had circumcised their children, they were executed according to the command. ^v60

###### v61
<span class="vn">61</span> And they hung the infants from their necks and their family, as well as the ones who circumcised them. ^v61

###### v62
<span class="vn">62</span> And many in Israel were in suspense and determined among themselves not to eat what was common. ^v62

###### v63
<span class="vn">63</span> And they chose to die so that they might not be defiled by the food and not profane the holy covenant, and they died. ^v63

###### v64
<span class="vn">64</span> And there came a very great wrath upon Israel. ^v64

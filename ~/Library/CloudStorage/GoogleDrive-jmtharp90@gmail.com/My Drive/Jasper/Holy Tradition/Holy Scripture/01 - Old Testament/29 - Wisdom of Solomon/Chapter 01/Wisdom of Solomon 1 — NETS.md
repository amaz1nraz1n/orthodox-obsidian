###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> There was a certain man in the land of Ausitis, whose name was Iob, and that man was genuine, blameless, righteous, religious, staying away from every evil thing. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Now there were born to him seven sons and three daughters. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> And his livestock was: seven thousand sheep, three thousand camels, five hundred yoke of oxen, five hundred she-donkeys at pasture; as well he had very many servants and extensive activities in the land, and that man was well-born among those of the east. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Now his sons used to gather with one another and hold a feast each day; they used to take along their three sisters as well, to eat and drink with them. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> And when the feast days had run their course, Iob used to send and purify them, rising early in the morning, and he used to offer a sacrifice on their behalf, according to their number, and one bull calf as a sin offering for their souls—for Iob said, “Perhaps my sons thought bad things in their mind toward God.” So this is what Iob would always do. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And when the set day came, then, look, the angels of God came to present themselves before the Lord, and the slanderer came with them. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> And the Lord said to the slanderer, “Where have you come from?” And the slanderer answered the Lord, “I have come, after going round the earth and walking about what is under heaven.” ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> And the Lord said to him, “Did you give thought to your disposition against my servant Iob—because there is no one of those on the earth like him, a man who is blameless, genuine, religious, staying away from every evil thing?” ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Then the slanderer answered and said before the Lord, “Does Iob really worship the Lord for nothing? ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Have you not put a fence around things external to him, both what is within his household as well as what is outside of all that belongs to him, all around? You blessed the works of his hands, and his livestock you increased on the earth. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> But stretch out your hand, and touch all that he has; surely he will bless you to your face!” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Then the Lord said to the slanderer, “Look, all that he has I am giving into your power, but do not touch him.” So the slanderer went out from the Lord. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> So it was, when it was the set day, Iob’s sons and daughters were drinking wine in their eldest brother’s house. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> And, look, a messenger came to Iob and said to him, “The yokes of oxen were plowing, and the she-donkeys were feeding beside them, ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> and marauders came and carried them off, and they killed the servants with daggers, but when I alone escaped, I came to tell you.” ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> While he was still speaking, a further messenger came and said to Iob, “Fire fell from the sky and burned up the sheep, and it likewise consumed the shepherds, and when I alone escaped, I came to tell you.” ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> While he was still speaking, a further messenger came and said to Iob, “Horsemen formed three columns against us, and they encircled the camels and carried them off and killed the servants with daggers, but I alone escaped, and I came to tell you.” ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> While he was still speaking, another messenger came, saying to Iob, “As your sons and daughters were eating and drinking at their elder brother’s, ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> suddenly a great wind came from the wilderness and struck the four corners of the house, and the house fell on your children, and they died, but I alone escaped, and I came to tell you.” ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> So Iob arose, tore his clothes and shaved the hair of his head and fell on the ground and did obeisance and said, ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> “I came naked from my mother’s womb; naked also shall I return there; the Lord gave; the Lord has taken away; as it seemed good to the Lord, so it turned out; blessed be the name of the Lord.” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> In all these things that happened to him Iob did not sin at all before the Lord, and he did not charge God with folly. ^v22

###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> And some, coming down from Judaea, taught the brothers that “Unless you are circumcised according to the custom of Moses you cannot be saved.” ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And, when Paul and bar-Nabas had no small measure of contention and argument with them, they appointed Paul and bar-Nabas, along with some others among them, to journey up to the Apostles and elders in Jerusalem concerning this question. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> So, having been sent on their way by the assembly, they passed through both Phoenicia and Samaria, relating at length how the gentiles had been converted and exciting great joy in all the brethren. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And, having arrived in Jerusalem, they were welcomed by the assembly and the Apostles and the elders, and reported the things that God had done with them. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> But some of those who had come to have faith who were from the sect of the Pharisees stood up from among them, saying: “It is necessary to circumcise them and charge them to keep the Law of Moses.” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> And the Apostles and the elders gathered together to look into this matter. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> And, when a great deal of examination had taken place, Peter rose and said to them, “Men, brothers, you are aware that, from days long past, God chose the gentiles among you to hear the word of the good tidings through my mouth, and to have faith. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> And the God who knows hearts bore witness, giving them the Spirit, the Holy One, just as he did us, ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And made no distinction between us and them, purifying their hearts through faith. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Why, therefore, do you now put God to the test, so as to place upon the neck of the disciples a yoke that neither our fathers nor we were strong enough to bear? ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> Rather, we have faith we shall be saved by the grace of the Lord Jesus, in the way that they too will be.” ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> And the whole company became silent and listened to bar-Nabas and Paul relate the signs and wonders God had performed among the gentiles through them. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> And, after remaining silent, James spoke up, saying, ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> “Men, brothers, listen to me. Simon has declared how God first saw to it that he would take a people for his name from the gentiles. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And the words of the prophets agree with this, just as has been written: ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> ‘“After these things, I will return and rebuild the fallen tabernacle of David, and will rebuild its ruins and erect it again, ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> So that the rest of humankind might seek out the Lord, even all the nations, those upon whom the name of the Lord has been invoked,” says the Lord who does these things, ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> Known from an age ago.’ ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Hence my verdict is not to cause difficulties for those among the gentiles turning to God, ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> But rather to write them, telling them to abstain from the pollutions of idols, and from whoring, and from anything strangled, and from blood. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> For Moses has men who preach him in every city, being read aloud in the synagogue every Sabbath since the times of generations long past.” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Then it was resolved by the Apostles and elders, together with the whole assembly, to send some men chosen from their midst—Judas (the one called bar-Sabbas) and Silas, leading men among the brethren—to Antioch along with Paul and bar-Nabas, ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> By whose hand they sent a letter: “The Apostles and the elder brethren send greetings to those brethren throughout Antioch and Syria and Cilicia who come from among the gentiles. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Since we have heard that some of our number, to whom we gave no orders, have troubled you with teachings unsettling to your souls, [saying you must be circumcised and be observant of the Law,] ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> It occurred to us, having reached a common accord, to send chosen men to you, along with our beloved bar-Nabas and Paul, ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Men having handed over their souls for the sake of the name of our Lord Jesus the Anointed. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> We have therefore sent Judas and Silas, and they will communicate the same to you verbally. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> For it was resolved by the Holy Spirit and by us to impose upon you no greater burden than these necessities: ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> To abstain from things sacrificed to idols and from blood and from things strangled and from whoring, by keeping yourselves from which you will be doing well. Farewell.” ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> They therefore, being dismissed, went down to Antioch and, assembling the community, delivered the letter. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> And having read it they were elated at the encouragement. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> And Judas and Silas, being themselves also prophets, encouraged and fortified the brethren with a good deal of teaching; ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> And when they had labored for a while they were dismissed by the brethren with a “Peace” to those who had sent them out. [ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> Nevertheless, it pleased Silas still to remain there.] ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> But Paul and bar-Nabas stayed in Antioch teaching and, along with many others, proclaiming the good tidings of the word of the Lord. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> And after some days Paul said to bar-Nabas, “Let us return, then, and visit the brethren throughout all the cities in which we announced the word of the Lord—how are they holding on?” ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> But bar-Nabas wished also to take along John (the one called Mark); ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> Paul did not think it fitting, however, to take someone along with them who had deserted them in Pamphylia and who had not accompanied them in their labor. ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> And it became a discord so sharp as to separate them from one another; and, taking Mark, bar-Nabas sailed off to Cyprus. ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> Having chosen Silas, however, Paul departed, commended by the brethren to the grace of the Lord; ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> And he went through Syria and Cilicia, fortifying the assemblies. ^v41

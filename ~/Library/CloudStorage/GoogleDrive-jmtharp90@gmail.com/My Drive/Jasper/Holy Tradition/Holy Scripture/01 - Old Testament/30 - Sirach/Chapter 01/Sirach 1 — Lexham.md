###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> All wisdom is from the Lord, and it is with him for eternity. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Who will count the sands of the seas and the drops of rain and the days of eternity? ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> Who will search out the height of heaven and the breadth of the earth, and the abyss and wisdom? ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Wisdom was created before everything, and prudent understanding from eternity.<sup class="nt-tn">[[Sirach 1 — Lexham Notes#^v4|*]]</sup> ^v4

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> To whom has the root of wisdom been revealed? And who knows her great deeds?<sup class="nt-tn">[[Sirach 1 — Lexham Notes#^v6|*]]</sup> ^v6

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> There is only one wise and very fearsome seated upon his throne. The Lord ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> himself created her and saw and measured her and poured her out upon all of his works, ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> together with all flesh, according to his gift; and he supplied her to those who love him. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> The fear of the Lord is glory and honor and gladness and a crown of joy. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> The fear of the Lord delights the heart and will give gladness and joy and length of days. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> The one who fears the Lord will be well at the end, and he will find grace on the day of his death. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> To fear God is the beginning of wisdom, and she was created in the womb along with the faithful. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> And she built a foundation of eternity with humans, and she will be entrusted with their seed. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> To fear the Lord is the fullness of wisdom, and she makes them drunk from her fruit. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> She will fill her whole house with desirable things, and the storehouses with her products. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> The fear of the Lord is a crown of wisdom, causing peace and health of healing to shoot up. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> And he saw and numbered her; he poured out skill and knowledge of comprehension like rain, and he exalted the glory of those who take possession of her. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> To fear the Lord is the root of wisdom, and her branches are length of days.<sup class="nt-tn">[[Sirach 1 — Lexham Notes#^v20|*]]</sup> ^v20

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Unrighteous wrath cannot be justified, for the tipping of his wrath is his fall. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> A patient man will withstand until an opportune time, and later, merriment will burst forth for him. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> He will hide his words until an opportune time, and the lips of the faithful will tell of his understanding. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> In the treasuries of wisdom is the parable of knowledge, but the fear of God is an abomination to a sinner. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Having desired wisdom, observe the commandments, and the Lord will supply it to you. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> For the fear of the Lord is wisdom and instruction, and his pleasure is faith and gentleness. ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> Do not resist the fear of the Lord, and do not come to him with a divided heart. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> Do not be a hypocrite in the mouths of people, and keep watch over your lips. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> Do not exalt yourself, lest you fall and bring dishonor to your soul, and the Lord will uncover your secrets, and he will throw you down in the middle of the congregation because you did not come in the fear of the Lord, and your heart was full of deceit. ^v30

*Job Curses His Day of Birth*
###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> After this, Job opened his mouth<sup class="nt-fn">[[Job 3 — OSB Notes#^f2143|†]]</sup> ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> and cursed his day, saying: ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> “May the day perish on which I was born, And the night in which they said, ‘Behold, a male child.’ ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> May that day be darkness, And may the Lord not regard it from above; Nor may the light come upon it. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> May darkness and the shadow of death seize it; May a cloud settle on it. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> May that day and night be cursed; May darkness carry it away; May it not come into the days of the year; May it not be numbered with the days of the month. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> But may that night be sorrow, And may no gladness come upon it, nor joy. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> But may he who curses that day curse it, Even he who is about to subdue the great sea-monster.<sup class="nt-fn">[[Job 3 — OSB Notes#^f2144|†]]</sup> ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> May the stars of that night be darkened, And may it remain dark. May it not come into the light, And may it not see the morning star arise; ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Because it did not shut up the doors of my mother's womb, For it would have removed sorrow from my eyes. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> Why did I not die in the womb? Why did I not perish immediately when I came from the womb? ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Why did the knees receive me, And why did I nurse the breasts? ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> For now I would have been lying down and quiet; I would have slept and been at rest ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> With kings and counselors of the earth Who were insolent with their swords, ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> Or with rulers who had abundant gold And filled their houses with silver; ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Or I would have been like an untimely birth Proceeding from his mother's womb, Or like infants who never saw the light. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> There the ungodly have burnt out the fury of wrath; There the weary in body are at rest. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> There men of ancient times have ceased together To hear the voice of those who collect taxes. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> The small and great are there, And the servant who feared his master. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Why is light given to those in bitterness, And life to souls in pain,<sup class="nt-fn">[[Job 3 — OSB Notes#^f2145|†]]</sup> ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Who long for death, but do not obtain it, Digging for it like treasures,<sup class="nt-fn">[[Job 3 — OSB Notes#^f2146|†]]</sup> ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> And would be very joyful if they could obtain it? ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Death is a rest to such a man, For God is against him. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> For groaning comes before my food; And I weep and am oppressed with terror. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> For the terror I was concerned about came to me, And what I feared happened to me; ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Neither was I at peace or quiet, nor at rest, For wrath came upon me.” ^v26

###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Jesus also told them a parable [illustrating] that they must always pray and not give up, ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> saying, “In a certain city, there was a judge who did not fear God and did not care [about anyone]. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> A widow lived in that city, and she often came to him, saying: ‘Defend me from my adversary!’ ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> For a time, he would not [do anything], but after a while, he said to himself, ‘Although I neither fear God nor care for anyone, ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> yet because this widow is bothering me, I will defend her, or else she will wear me out <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn859|†]]</sup> by her continual visits.’” ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> The Lord said, “Listen to what the unrighteous judge has to say! ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Will not God grant justice to his elect who are crying out to him day and night, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn860|†]]</sup> although he exercises patience regarding them? <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn861|†]]</sup> ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> I tell you that he will avenge them quickly! And yet, when the Son of Man comes, will he find faith <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn862|†]]</sup> on the land?” <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn863|†]]</sup> ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> Jesus <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn864|†]]</sup> also spoke this parable to certain people who were convinced of their own righteousness and who despised everyone else. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> “Two men went up into the temple to pray; one was a Pharisee, and the other was a Publican. <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn865|†]]</sup> ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> The Pharisee, standing by himself, prayed like this: ‘God, I thank you that I am not like the rest of men, dishonest, unrighteous, adulterers, or even like this tax collector. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> I fast twice a week; I give tithes of all that I earn.’ ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> But the tax collector, standing far aside, would not even lift up his eyes to heaven. Instead, he beat his breast, saying, ‘God, be merciful to me, a <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn866|†]]</sup> sinner!’ ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> I tell you, this man, rather than the other, went down to his house justified. For everyone who exalts himself will be humbled, but the one who humbles himself will be exalted.” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> People were also bringing their infants to him, so that Jesus might lay hands <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn867|†]]</sup> on them. But when the disciples saw this, they rebuked them. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Jesus summoned his disciples and told them, “Allow the little children to come to me, and do not hinder them, for the Kingdom of God belongs to such as these. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Amen, I tell you: whoever does not receive the Kingdom of God like a little child will in no way enter into it.” ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> A certain ruler <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn868|†]]</sup> asked him, “Good Teacher, what shall <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn869|†]]</sup> I do to inherit eternal life?” ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Jesus asked him, “Why do you call me good? No one is good, <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn870|†]]</sup> except one—God. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> You know the commandments: ‘Do not commit adultery,’ ‘Do not murder,’ ‘Do not steal,’ ‘Do not bear false witness,’ ‘Honor your father and your mother.’” <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn871|†]]</sup> ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> The ruler replied, “I have observed all these things from my youth.” ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> When Jesus heard these things, he said, “You still lack one thing. Sell all that you have, distribute it to the poor, and you will have a treasure in heaven. Then come, follow me.” ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> But when the man heard these things, he became very distressed because he was very rich. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Jesus, seeing that he had become very distressed, said, “How hard it is for those who have wealth to enter into the Kingdom of God! ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> Indeed, it is easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle than for someone who is wealthy to enter into the Kingdom of God!” ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Those who heard this exclaimed, “Then who can be saved?” ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> But Jesus replied, “The things which are impossible with men are possible with God.” ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> Peter said, “Look, we have left everything and followed you!” ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> And Jesus said to them, “Amen, I tell you: there is no one who has left house, or wife, or brothers, or parents, or children, for the sake of the Kingdom of God, ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> who will not receive many times more in this [present] time, and in the age to come, eternal life.” ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> He took the Twelve aside, and said to them, “Behold, we are going up to Jerusalem, and all the things that are written through the prophets concerning the Son of Man will be fulfilled. ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> For he will be delivered to the Gentiles, mocked, mistreated, and spit upon. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Having flogged him, they will kill him, and on the third day, he will rise again.” ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> However, they understood none of these things. What Jesus was saying was concealed from them, and they did not understand the things that were said. ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> As Jesus <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn872|†]]</sup> was approaching Jericho, a certain blind man sat by the road, begging. ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> Hearing a crowd going by, he asked what this meant. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> People told him that Jesus of Nazareth was passing by. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> He began to cry out, “Jesus, son of David, have mercy on me!” ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Then those who led the way rebuked him, ordering him to be quiet. But the blind man <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn873|†]]</sup> cried out all the more, “Son of David, have mercy on me!” ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> Standing still, Jesus commanded that the blind man <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn874|†]]</sup> be brought to him. When the man had come near, Jesus asked him, ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> ”What do you want me to do?” The man replied, “Lord, that I may see again!” ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> Jesus said to him, “Receive your sight. Your faith has healed <sup class="nt-fn">[[Luke 18 — EOB Notes#^edn875|†]]</sup> you.” ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> Immediately, the man received his sight and began to follow Jesus, glorifying God. When all the people saw this, they praised God. ^v43

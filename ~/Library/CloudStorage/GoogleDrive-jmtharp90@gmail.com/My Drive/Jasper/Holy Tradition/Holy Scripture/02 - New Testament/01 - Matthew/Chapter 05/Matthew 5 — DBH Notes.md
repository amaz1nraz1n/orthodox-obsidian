### [[Matthew 5#v3|5:3]]
^v3
> [!tn] 5:3 ^fn394
> μακάριος (*makarios*): “blessed,” “happy,” “fortunate,” “prosperous,” but originally with a connotation of divine or heavenly bliss.

> [!tn] 5:3 ^fn393
> A πτωχός (*ptōchos*) is a poor man or beggar, but with the connotation of one who is abject: cowering or cringing.

### [[Matthew 5#v22|5:22]]
^v22
> [!tn] 5:22 ^fn392
> ῥακά (h*raka*): an Aramaic word meaning “foolish,” “empty,” “of no account,” “worthless.”

> [!tn] 5:22 ^fn391
> μωρός (*mōros*): “foolish,” “insipid,” “dull,” “of no account”; occasionally with the connotation of low and deplorable character.

### [[Matthew 5#v28|5:28]]
^v28
> [!tn] 5:28 ^fn390
> γυνή (*gynē*): “woman” (as distinct from “maiden,” “virgin”), “wife.” Here the topic is clearly a man who wishes to violate the marriage covenant of another man’s wife.

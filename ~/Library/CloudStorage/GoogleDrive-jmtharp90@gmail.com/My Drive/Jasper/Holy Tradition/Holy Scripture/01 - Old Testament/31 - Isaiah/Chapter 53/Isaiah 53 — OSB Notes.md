### [[Isaiah 53#v2|53:2]]
^v2
> [!note] 53:2 ^f3521
> **He had no form or beauty: **“He sat upon the foal of an ass; He was despised among the people and humbled Himself even unto death” (Iren).

### [[Isaiah 53#v3|53:3-4]]
^v3
> [!note] 53:3-4 ^f3522
> God became **dishonored**, for Christ assumed a true human nature. “O marvel at the loving kindness of the Word, that for our sakes He is dishonored, that we may be brought to honor” (AthanG).

### [[Isaiah 53#v5|53:5]]
^v5
> [!note] 53:5 ^f3523
> Christ, who was free from sin, took our **chastisement** and gave us **peace.** By **His bruise **we are saved from an eternity of suffering and estrangement from God.

### [[Isaiah 53#v6|53:6]]
^v6
> [!note] 53:6 ^f3524
> **The Lord**, God the Father, **delivered Him**, God the Son, to die **for our sins**. This is the message St. Paul proclaims in [[II Corinthians 5#v21|2Co 5:21]].

### [[Isaiah 53#v7|53:7]]
^v7
> [!note] 53:7 ^f3525
> “The shepherd of Israel, and now of the whole world also” (GrgNa) became “the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world” ([[John 1#v29|Jn 1:29]]).

### [[Isaiah 53#v8|53:8]]
^v8
> [!note] 53:8 ^f3526
> Christ **was led to death**, yet death could not hold His perfect, sinless body (see [[Acts 2#v24|Acts 2:24]]).

### [[Isaiah 53#v9|53:9]]
^v9
> [!note] 53:9 ^f3527
> **He committed no lawlessness: **See [[Hebrews 4#v15|Heb 4:15]].

### [[Isaiah 53#v10|53:10]]
^v10
> [!note] 53:10 ^f3528
> **The Lord**, God the Father, cleansed **Him**, God the Son, **of His wound** because His body could not remain subject to corruption and death. The **offering** made by Christ enables man to receive the same healing and eternal life.

### [[Isaiah 53#v12|53:12]]
^v12
> [!note] 53:12 ^f3529
> **He was considered among the lawless **is a reference to “the robbers who were crucified with Him. . . . Both of them were before this transgressors, but one was so no longer” (CyrJer).

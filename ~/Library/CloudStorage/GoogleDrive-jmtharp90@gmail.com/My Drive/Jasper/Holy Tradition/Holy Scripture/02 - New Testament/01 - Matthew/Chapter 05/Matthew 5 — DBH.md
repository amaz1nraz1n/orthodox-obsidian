> **Nav:** [[Matthew 5|Hub]] · [[Matthew 5 — DBH Notes|DBH Notes]] · [[Matthew 5 — NET Notes|NET Notes]]

###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Now, seeing the crowds, he ascended the mountain; and when he seated himself his disciples approached him; ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> And opening his mouth he taught them, saying: ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> “How blissful<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 5 — DBH Notes#^fn394|†]]</sup> the destitute, abject<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 5 — DBH Notes#^fn393|†]]</sup> in spirit, for theirs is the Kingdom of the heavens; ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> How blissful those who mourn, for they shall be aided; ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> How blissful the gentle, for they shall inherit the earth; ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> How blissful those who hunger and thirst for what is right, for they shall feast; ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> How blissful the merciful, for they shall receive mercy; ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> How blissful the pure in heart, for they shall see God; ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> How blissful the peacemakers, for they shall be called sons of God; ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> How blissful those who have been persecuted for the sake of what is right, for theirs is the Kingdom of the heavens; ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> How blissful you when they reproach you, and persecute you and falsely accuse you of every evil for my sake: ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> Rejoice and be glad, for your reward in the heavens is great; for thus they persecuted the prophets before you. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> “You are the salt of the earth; but if the salt should become insipid, by what shall it be made salty? It is no longer of any use except to scatter outside for people to tread upon. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> You are the light of the world. A city set upon a hill cannot be hidden; ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> Neither do they light a lamp and place it under the dry-goods basket, but rather they place it upon a lampstand, and it illumines all who are in the house. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> So let your light shine out before humanity, so that they may see your good works and may glorify your Father in the heavens. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> “Do not think that I came to destroy the Law and the prophets; I came not to destroy but to fulfill. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> For, amen, I tell you, until heaven and earth shall pass away, not a single iota or single serif must vanish from the Law, until all things come to pass. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Whoever breaks one of the least of the commandments and teaches people to do likewise shall be called least in the Kingdom of the heavens; but whoever performs and teaches it, this one shall be called great in the Kingdom of the heavens. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For I tell you that, unless your uprightness surpass that of the Scribes and Pharisees, you shall not enter into the Kingdom of the heavens. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> “You have heard that it was said to those of ancient times: ‘You shall not commit murder; and whoever commits murder shall be liable to judgment.’ ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Whereas I say to you that everyone who becomes angry with his brother shall be liable to judgment; and whoever says ‘*Raka*’<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 5 — DBH Notes#^fn392|†]]</sup> to his brother shall be liable to the Council; and whoever says ‘worthless reprobate’<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 5 — DBH Notes#^fn391|†]]</sup> shall be liable to enter Hinnom’s Vale of fire. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> If, therefore, you bring your gift to the altar and there recall that your brother holds something against you, ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Leave your gift in front of the altar, and first go and be reconciled with your brother, and then come and offer your gift. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> Be quick to show good will to the plaintiff against you, while you are out in the street with him, lest that plaintiff deliver you to the judge, and the judge to the guard, and you are thrown into prison. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Amen, I tell you, you shall most certainly not emerge from there until you repay the very last pittance. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> “You have heard it said, ‘You shall not commit adultery.’ ^v27

###### v28
<span class="vn">28</span> Whereas I tell you that everyone looking at a married woman<sup class="nt-fn">[[Matthew 5 — DBH Notes#^fn390|†]]</sup> in order to lust after her has already committed adultery with her in his heart. ^v28

###### v29
<span class="vn">29</span> So if your right eye causes you to falter remove it and fling it away from you; for it is expedient for you that one of your members should perish, rather than that your whole body should be thrown into the Vale of Hinnom. ^v29

###### v30
<span class="vn">30</span> And, if your right hand causes you to falter, cut it off and fling it away from you; for it is expedient for you that one of your members should perish, rather than that your whole body should depart into the Vale of Hinnom. ^v30

###### v31
<span class="vn">31</span> Moreover, it has been said, ‘Whoever divorces his wife, he must provide her with a writ of separation.’ ^v31

###### v32
<span class="vn">32</span> Whereas I tell you that everyone who divorces his wife, except in cases of whorishness, causes her to commit adultery, and whoever weds a divorced woman commits adultery. ^v32

###### v33
<span class="vn">33</span> Again, you have heard that it was said to those of ancient times, ‘You shall not swear oaths falsely,’ and ‘You shall render up to the Lord what your oaths are sworn upon.’ ^v33

###### v34
<span class="vn">34</span> Whereas I tell you not to swear at all: neither by heaven, inasmuch as it is God’s throne; ^v34

###### v35
<span class="vn">35</span> Nor by the earth, inasmuch as it is the footstool of his feet; nor by Jerusalem, inasmuch as it is the Great King’s city; ^v35

###### v36
<span class="vn">36</span> —Neither swear by your own head, inasmuch as you cannot make a single hair white or black. ^v36

###### v37
<span class="vn">37</span> Rather, let your utterance be ‘Yes, yes,’ ‘No, no’; because it is from the roguish man that anything more extravagant than this comes. ^v37

###### v38
<span class="vn">38</span> You have heard that it was said, ‘An eye for an eye and a tooth for a tooth.’ ^v38

###### v39
<span class="vn">39</span> Whereas I tell you not to oppose the wicked man by force; rather, whosoever strikes you upon the right cheek, turn to him the other as well; ^v39

###### v40
<span class="vn">40</span> And to him who wishes to bring a judgment against you, so he may take away your tunic, give him your cloak as well; ^v40

###### v41
<span class="vn">41</span> And whoever presses you into service for one mile, go with him for two. ^v41

###### v42
<span class="vn">42</span> Give to the one who begs from you, and do not turn away from one who wishes to borrow from you. ^v42

###### v43
<span class="vn">43</span> You have heard that it has been said, ‘You shall love your neighbor and shall hate your enemy’— ^v43

###### v44
<span class="vn">44</span> Whereas I tell you, love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you; ^v44

###### v45
<span class="vn">45</span> In this way you may become sons of your Father in the heavens, for he makes his sun to rise on the wicked and the good, and sends rain upon the just and the unjust. ^v45

###### v46
<span class="vn">46</span> For if you love only those who love you, what recompense do you have? Do not even the tax-collectors do the same? ^v46

###### v47
<span class="vn">47</span> And if you greet only your brothers, what are you doing that is extraordinary? Do not even the gentiles do the same? ^v47

###### v48
<span class="vn">48</span> So be perfect, as your Heavenly Father is perfect. ^v48

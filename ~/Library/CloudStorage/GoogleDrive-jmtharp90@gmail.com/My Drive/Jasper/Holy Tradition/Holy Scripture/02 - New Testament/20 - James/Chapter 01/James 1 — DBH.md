###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> James, a slave of God and of Lord Jesus the Anointed, to the twelve tribes in the Diaspora: Greetings. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> Consider it all joy, my brothers, whenever you might fall into various trials, ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> Knowing that the testing of your faithfulness produces perseverance. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> And let perseverance have its operation in full, so that you may be perfect and whole, lacking nothing. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> But, if any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask for it from the God who gives to all unreservedly and without reproach, and it will be given to him. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> But let him ask in faith, not hesitating, for he who hesitates is like a wave of the sea driven by the wind and tossed about. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> For let that man not presume he will receive anything from the Lord: ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> A man divided in soul, fickle in all his ways. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> And let the lowly brother exult in his elevation, ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> But the rich man in his abasement, because he will pass away like a flower in the grass; ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> For the sun rose with a scorching heat and withered the grass, and its flower fell away, and the loveliness of its face perished; thus also will the rich man fade away amid his undertakings. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> How blissful the man who endures trial, because—having become proven—he will receive the crown of the life that he has promised to those who love him. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Let no one who is being tempted say, “I am being tempted by God”; for God is incapable of temptation by evil things, and himself tempts no one. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> But everyone is tempted by his own desire, being drawn away and enticed; ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> This desire, having conceived, gives birth to sin, and sin fully grown bears death as its offspring. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Do not go astray, my beloved brothers. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> Every good act of giving and every perfect gift is from above, descending from the Father of the Luminaries, with whom there is no alternation or shadow of change. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> Having so resolved, he gave birth to us by a word of truth, so that we should be a kind of firstfruits from among his creatures. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Know this, my beloved brothers: Let every man be swift to listen, slow to speak, slow to indignation; ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For a human being’s indignation does not accomplish God’s justice. ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> Hence, putting away every defilement and surfeit of evil, receive in gentleness the implanted word, which can save your souls. ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> And become doers of the word, and not only hearers, thus deluding yourselves. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Because, if anyone is a hearer of the word and not a doer, he is like a man observing the face he was born with in a mirror; ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> Because he has observed himself and gone away, and has immediately forgotten what he was like. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> For the one who has gazed intently into the perfect law, which is one of freedom, and has stayed there next to it, becoming not a forgetful listener but instead a doer of work—this one will be blissful in what he does. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> If anyone fancies himself religious while not bridling his tongue, but instead deceiving his own heart, his religion is empty. ^v26

###### v27
<span class="vn">27</span> Pure and undefiled religion before the God and Father is this: to watch over orphans and widows in their affliction, to keep oneself unstained by the cosmos. ^v27

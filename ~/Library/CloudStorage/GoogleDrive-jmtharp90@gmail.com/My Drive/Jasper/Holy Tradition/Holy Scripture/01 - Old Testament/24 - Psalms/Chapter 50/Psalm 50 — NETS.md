###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Regarding completion. A Psalm. Pertaining to Dauid. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> When the prophet Nathan came to him, after he had gone into Bersabee. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> Have mercy on me, O God, according to your great mercy, and according to the abundance of your compassion blot out my lawless deed. ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Wash me thoroughly from my lawlessness, and from my sin cleanse me, ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> because my lawlessness I know and my sin is ever before me. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Against you alone did I sin, and what is evil before you I did, so that you may be justified in your words and be victorious when you go to law. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> For, look, I was conceived in lawlessness, and in sin did my mother crave for me. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> For, look, you loved truth; the unclear and secret aspects of your wisdom you made clear to me. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> You will sprinkle me with hyssop, and I shall be cleansed; you will wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> You will make me hear joy and gladness; humbled bones will rejoice. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> Turn away your face from my sins, and all my lawless acts blot out. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> A clean heart create in me, O God, and an upright spirit renew within me. ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Do not cast me away from your face, and your holy spirit do not take from me. ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> Restore to me the joy of your deliverance, and with a leading spirit support me. ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> I will teach lawless ones your ways, and impious ones will return to you. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Rescue me from bloodshed, O God, O God of my deliverance; my tongue will rejoice at your righteousness. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> O Lord, my lips you will open, and my mouth will declare your praise, ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> because if you had wanted sacrifice, I would have given it; with whole burnt offerings you will not be pleased. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Sacrifice to God is a broken spirit; a broken and humbled heart God will not despise. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> Do good to Sion in your good pleasure, and let the walls of Ierousalem be built; ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> then you will delight in a sacrifice of righteousness, in offering and whole burnt offerings; then they will offer calves on your altar. ^v21

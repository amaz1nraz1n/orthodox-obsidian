###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> After this Job opened his mouth and cursed his day, ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> saying, ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> “May the day perish in which I was brought forth, and that night when they said, ‘Behold, a male!’ ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> Let that night be darkness! And let not the Lord investigate it from above, nor let light come upon it. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> But let gloom and a shadow of death seize it. Let darkness come upon it. May the day be cursed; ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> also that night. Let darkness carry it off. Neither let it be as a day of the year, nor numbered as a day of the month. ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> But may that night be pain, and may neither merriment nor joy come on it. ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> But may he who curses that day curse it, he who is about to subdue the great fish. ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> May the star of that night be darkened, may it wait and not come to illumination, and may it not see the morning star rise. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> Because it did not close the opening of my mother’s womb, for it might remove grief from my eyes. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> On what account did I not die in the belly, and why did I come forth from the womb and not immediately be destroyed? ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> For what purpose did the knees happen to me? And for what purpose did I suck breasts? ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> Now falling asleep I am still, and sleeping I rest ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> with kings and counselors of the earth, who were proud of their swords, ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> or with rulers who had much gold, who filled their houses with silver. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Or I should have been like an untimely birth coming from the womb of a mother, or as an infant who never sees light. ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> There the wicked have burnt out their wrathful anger; there the weary in body rest. ^v17

###### v18
<span class="vn">18</span> But the eternal ones, together, hear not the voice of the tribute collector. ^v18

###### v19
<span class="vn">19</span> Small and great are there, and an attendant who feared his lord. ^v19

###### v20
<span class="vn">20</span> For to what purpose has light been given to those who are in bitterness, and life to those who are in spiritual pain, ^v20

###### v21
<span class="vn">21</span> who long for death and do not find it, digging for it like treasure? ^v21

###### v22
<span class="vn">22</span> Yet they would be exceedingly glad if they should be successful. ^v22

###### v23
<span class="vn">23</span> Death to such a man is rest, for God has enclosed him. ^v23

###### v24
<span class="vn">24</span> For groaning comes before my bread, and I weep as I gather myself in fear. ^v24

###### v25
<span class="vn">25</span> For the fear that I focused upon has come upon me, and what I had feared has fallen upon me. ^v25

###### v26
<span class="vn">26</span> Neither did I live in peace, nor did I have quiet, nor was I at rest, but anger came to me.” ^v26

### [[Psalm 1#v1|1:1]]
^v1
> [!note] 1:1 ^f1997
> The Man in [[Psalms 1#v1|Ps 1]] is the Lord Jesus Christ (JohnChr, AmbM, Eust, CyrAl, Aug, and Cass). Thus, the Incarnation sets the tone of Psalms, and in the Psalms the Incarnate Lord teaches His Church how to pray. This is why Psalms is the prayer book of the Church.
>
> [[Psalms 1#v1|Ps 1]] presents how Jesus lived His life in this world by showing the contrast between His godly life and the life of the ungodly. The Church, then, is to follow His example: His behavior in relation to the ungodly (v. [[Psalms 1#v1|1]]); His zeal for the truth (v. [[Psalms 1#v2|2]]); and His holy and virtuous life (v. [[Psalms 1#v3|3]]).

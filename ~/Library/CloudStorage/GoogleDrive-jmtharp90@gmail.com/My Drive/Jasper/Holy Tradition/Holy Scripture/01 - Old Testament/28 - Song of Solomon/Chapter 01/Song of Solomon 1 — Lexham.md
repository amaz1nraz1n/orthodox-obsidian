###### v1
<span class="vn">1</span> Song of songs, which is Solomon’s. I am a flower of the plain, a lily of the hollows. On my bed at night<sup class="nt-alt">[[Song of Solomon 1 — Lexham Notes#^v1|⁺]]</sup> I sought him whom my soul loved. I sought him, and I did not find him; I called him, and he did not listen to me. ^v1

###### v2
<span class="vn">2</span> May he kiss me with kisses of his mouth! For your breasts are better than wine, Like a lily in the midst of thorns, so is my close one between the daughters. I will get up then and go around the city, in the marketplaces and in the streets, and I will seek him whom my soul loved. I sought him, and I did not find him. ^v2

###### v3
<span class="vn">3</span> and the scent of your ointment better than all spices; your name is an ointment poured out. On account of this, young women have fallen in love with you. Like an apple tree among the trees of the wood, so is my beloved between the sons. In his shade I have felt desire and sat down, and his fruit is sweet in my throat. The watchmen found me, those who go around in the city: “Have you not seen him whom my soul loved?” ^v3

###### v4
<span class="vn">4</span> They drew you away. We will run after you following the scent of your ointments. The king has brought me into his chamber. Let us exult and make merry with you. We will love your breasts more than wine. Uprightness has loved you. Bring me into the house of wine; array love over me. How little time it was after I went away from them before I found him whom my soul loved. I took hold of him, and I did not let him loose until they brought him into my mother’s house and into the chamber of she who conceived me. ^v4

###### v5
<span class="vn">5</span> I am black and beautiful, daughters of Israel, like the tents of Kedar, like the leather curtains of Solomon. Strengthen me with perfumes, overwhelm me with apples, for I am wounded by love. I made you swear, daughters of Jerusalem, by the powers and by the forces of the field: Cursed if you awaken and rouse love until it wants. ^v5

###### v6
<span class="vn">6</span> Do not look upon me, for I am blackened because the sun has looked down upon me. The sons of my mother fought with me. They made me a guard in the vineyards; I have not guarded my vineyard. His left hand is under my head, and his right hand will embrace me. Who is this woman coming up from the desert like a column of smoke, perfumed with myrrh and frankincense, from all the powders of a perfumer? ^v6

###### v7
<span class="vn">7</span> Tell me, you whom my soul has loved: Where do you tend flocks? Where do you bring them to rest at noon? lest I become like a woman who clothes herself near a herd of your companions. I made you swear, daughters of Jerusalem, by powers and by forces of the field: Cursed if you awaken and rouse love until it wants. Look! The bed of Solomon, sixty mighty ones round about it, of the mighty ones of Israel, ^v7

###### v8
<span class="vn">8</span> If you do not know yourself, O most beautiful of women,<sup class="nt-alt">[[Song of Solomon 1 — Lexham Notes#^v8|⁺]]</sup> go out in the footsteps of the flocks and tend your goats at the tents of the shepherds. The voice of my beloved! Look! He has come, leaping over the mountains, jumping over the hills. all of them holding a sword, instructed in battle; as for each man, his sword is upon his thigh because of the terror at night.<sup class="nt-alt">[[Song of Solomon 1 — Lexham Notes#^v8|⁺]]</sup> ^v8

###### v9
<span class="vn">9</span> To my mare amongst the chariot horses of Pharaoh I have compared you, my close one. My beloved is like a gazelle or a deer fawn upon the mountains of Bethel. Look! He is behind our wall, looking through the windows, peeping out through the lattices. King Solomon made a sedan chair for himself from the trees of Lebanon. ^v9

###### v10
<span class="vn">10</span> How lovely have your cheeks become, like turtle doves, your neck like necklaces! My beloved replies and says to me, “Rise! Come, my close one, my beautiful one, my dove! He made its pillars silver and its back golden; its steps are purple; inside of it is a mosaic, as love from the daughters of Jerusalem. ^v10

###### v11
<span class="vn">11</span> We will make images of gold for you with studs of silver. For look, the winter has departed; the rain has gone—it went away by itself. Come out and look at King Solomon in the crown with which his mother crowned him on the day of his wedding and on the day of the merriment of his heart. ^v11

###### v12
<span class="vn">12</span> While the king was reclining, my nard gave off its scent. The flowers have appeared on the earth, the time of pruning has arrived, the voice of the turtledove has been heard in our land; ^v12

###### v13
<span class="vn">13</span> My beloved is a bundle of myrrh for me the fig tree has borne its figs, the vines are blossoming, they have given off a scent. Rise! Come, my close one, my beautiful one, my dove, and come, ^v13

###### v14
<span class="vn">14</span> in the vineyards of En-gedi. you my dove, under the shelter of the rock, near the outer wall; show me your face, and let me hear your voice, for your voice is pleasant, and your face is lovely.” ^v14

###### v15
<span class="vn">15</span> Look! You are beautiful, my close one. Look! You are beautiful; your eyes are doves. Catch the foxes for us, the little ones destroying the vineyards; and our vines are blossoming. ^v15

###### v16
<span class="vn">16</span> Look! You are beautiful, my beloved, and lovely, too! What’s more, our bed is shaded. My beloved is mine, and I am his; he who tends flocks among the lilies ^v16

###### v17
<span class="vn">17</span> The beams of our houses are cedar trees; our rafters are cypress trees. until the day blows away, and the shadows move. Return! Beloved, make yourself like the gazelle or a deer fawn upon the mountains with hollows. ^v17

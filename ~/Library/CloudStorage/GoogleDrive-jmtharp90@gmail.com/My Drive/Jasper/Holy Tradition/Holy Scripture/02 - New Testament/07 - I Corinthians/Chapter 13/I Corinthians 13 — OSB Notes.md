### [[I Corinthians 13#v1|13:1]]
^v1
> [!note] 13:1 ^f5453
> St. Basil the Great teaches there are three reasons, all biblical and all God-given, that people follow the Lord: (1) fear ([[Proverbs 1#v7|Pr 1:7]]); (2) obedience, a recognition that because He is God we must follow Him ([[Acts 5#v29|Acts 5:29]]); and (3) **love** ([[I John 4#v19|1Jn 4:19]]). And while we must aim to do all things out of love, we also must not cease fearing God and obeying Him. To a church preoccupied with **tongues**, Paul teaches the ultimate gift: love.

### [[I Corinthians 13#v2|13:2]]
^v2
> [!note] 13:2 ^f5454
> **Love** outshines **prophecy** and **all faith**. It is of far greater value than the miraculous ability to **remove mountains**.

### [[I Corinthians 13#v3|13:3]]
^v3
> [!note] 13:3 ^f5455
> Jesus urges us to “give to the poor” ([[Matthew 19#v21|Mt 19:21]]) and to lay down our lives for others ([[John 15#v13|Jn 15:13]]). But even such a selfless act done without **love profits** us **nothing**.

> [!info] 13:3a ^fvar558
> NU-Text reads *so I may boast.*

### [[I Corinthians 13#v4|13:4-8]]
^v4
> [!note] 13:4-8 ^f5456
> How do we know we are acting with **love** (v. [[I Corinthians 13#v4|4]])? This passage gives specific characteristics of love: (1) it **suffers long**, is patient ([[I Thessalonians 5#v14|1Th 5:14]]); (2) it is **kind**, gentle, especially with those who hurt ([[Ephesians 4#v32|Eph 4:32]]); (3) it **does not envy**, is not jealous of what others have ([[Proverbs 23#v17|Pr 23:17]]); (4) it **does not parade itself**, put itself on display ([[John 3#v30|Jn 3:30]]); (5) it **is not puffed up**, arrogant, proud ([[Galatians 6#v3|Gal 6:3]]); (6) it **does not** act **rudely**, brashly, mean-spiritedly, insulting others ([[Ecclesiastes 5#v2|Ecc 5:2]]); (7) it **does not seek its own** way, act pushy ([[I Corinthians 10#v24|10:24]]); (8) it **is not provoked** or angered ([[Proverbs 19#v11|Pr 19:11]]); (9) it **thinks no evil**, does not keep score on others ([[Hebrews 10#v17|Heb 10:17]]); (10) it rejoices not **in iniquity**, takes no pleasure when others fall into sin ([[Mark 3#v5|Mk 3:5]]); (11) it **rejoices in the truth**, is joyful when righteousness prevails ([[II John 1#v4|2Jn 4]]); (12) it **bears all things**, handles the burdensome ([[Galatians 6#v2|Gal 6:2]]); (13) it **believes all things**, trusts in God no matter what ([[Proverbs 3#v5|Pr 3:5]]); (14) it **hopes all things**, keeps looking up, does not despair ([[Philippians 3#v13|Php 3:13]]); (15) it **endures all things**, “puts up with everything” (JohnChr), does not wear out ([[Galatians 6#v9|Gal 6:9]]); (16) it **never fails**. The only thing love cannot do is fail ([[I Corinthians 16#v14|16:14]]).

### [[I Corinthians 13#v8|13:8-13]]
^v8
> [!note] 13:8-13 ^f5457
> The gifts of the Spirit, wonderful as they are, are temporary and incomplete. They are for this age, while we are “children” (v. [[I Corinthians 13#v11|11]]). But **love** continues into the age to come: it is eternal, complete, and fulfilling.

### [[I Corinthians 13#v11|13:11]]
^v11
> [!note] 13:11 ^f5458
> The Scriptures differentiate between being dependent on God, or childlike ([[Matthew 18#v3|Mt 18:3]]), and being **childish** or immature.
